# Poaching, Black Markets, and Illegal Control Tech

Defines how illegal capture works in Amber continuity, why it’s different from lawful training, and how black markets intersect with Team Rocket and other buyers.

## Core Definitions

- Poaching --- catching, transporting, or selling Pokémon in violation of law (protected zones, permitless taking, forged registration) or ethics (force methods that prevent legal registration and control).
- Black market --- informal networks that fence stolen balls, sell illegally taken Pokémon, and traffic control devices. Operate around Celadon backstreets, Vermilion docks, and route roadhouses.

## Legal Capture vs Poaching

- Ball Handshake (legal):
  - A Poké Ball must perform a registration handshake with a conscious or at‑least responsive target. The ball records species biometrics and assigns ownership to the trainer license.
  - Consent model: not literal spoken consent, but the Pokémon must not be fully collapsed; resistance and struggle are normal, but a completely fainted target cannot complete the handshake.

- Fainted Pokémon Rule:
  - Fully fainted Pokémon cannot be returned to their ball and cannot be newly captured. Their ability to dematerialize is offline until stabilized. See mechanics/pokemon-fainting.md.

- Poaching Methods (illegal):
  - Blunt‑force knockouts or drug sedation to absolute collapse, then physical cages. No valid handshake occurs.
  - Forged registry entries or cloned serial numbers on balls to simulate ownership.
  - Capture in protected zones or closed seasons.

- Practical Difference:
  - Lawful trainers must prove control and welfare; their balls pass registry checks and the Pokémon responds to standard handling.
  - Poachers cannot reliably control stock; animals frequently injure handlers or relapse when drugs wear off.

## Team Rocket’s Role

- Largest regional buyer and logistics hub for poached Pokémon.
- Channels: Game Corner adjacencies, shell importers at docks, and “equipment suppliers” for clone balls and jammers.
- Diversified crime: extortion, theft rings, industrial sabotage, item fencing, loan‑sharking.

## Illegal Control Technologies (Contraband)

- Shock harnesses and pain collars --- remote compliance via aversive conditioning; illegal for commercial sale or field use.
- Psionic dampeners --- headbands/muzzles that suppress Psychic output; illegal without medical exemption.
- Neurochemical drip rigs --- sedative micro‑pumps to keep a target sub‑responsive; treated as animal cruelty.
- Recall jammers --- block legal recalls during ambushes; often paired with Faraday crates.
- Clone balls/serial spoofers --- duplicate ball IDs or simulate registry writes; defeated by rolling‑code audits.

Rangers and police seize these items on sight; possession implies trafficking intent.

## Enforcement & Penalties

- Ranger Union focus: protected zones, route perimeters, and transport hubs. See organizations/ranger-union.md.
- Penalties scale by harm: fines, license suspension, ball confiscation, and incarceration for organized trafficking or cruelty.
- Civil liability: damages to gyms, Centers, or victims; insurers subrogate aggressively.

## Rescue & Rehabilitation

- Fainted/abused Pokémon require stabilization before any legal custody decision. Centers document chain‑of‑care; Rangers seek original lawful owners when possible.
- Court outcomes: return to lawful owner, sanctuary placement, or re‑taming under certified programs. Poached stock cannot be laundered through fresh registration.

## Author Notes (Use On‑Screen)

- Show the difference: lawful capture scenes emphasize management, commands, and post‑battle care; poaching scenes show cages, sedatives, and failed recalls.
- Stakes: fainted targets can’t “escape into a ball,” making rescues urgent.
- Tech texture: drop a jammer or collar detail sparingly; avoid gadget sprawl.

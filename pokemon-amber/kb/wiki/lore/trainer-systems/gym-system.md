# Gym Tier System (Pillar vs Minor)

Kanto's gym network is divided into two operational tiers: League-recognized pillar gyms and non-pillar minor gyms. Both can issue valid badges, but they differ in governance standards, appointment pressure, and challenge structure.

## Overview

The pillar/minor distinction separates public prestige and institutional obligations from local training access. Pillar gyms anchor League certification and city-level public service, while minor gyms expand coverage and provide alternative progression routes. In practice, pillar gyms optimize prestige and standardization --- minor gyms optimize accessibility and instructional flexibility.

## Pillar Gyms

Pillar gyms are official League-recognized institutions with city-backed operating support.

**Defining traits:**
- Formal League recognition and regulatory oversight
- City funding or city-backed infrastructure support
- High badge prestige for public reputation and tournament seeding
- Stricter challenge administration and safety standards
- Stronger expectation to maintain broad public legitimacy

Because pillar badges carry higher institutional weight, pillar gyms typically enforce tighter scheduling, clearer retake gates, and more formal challenge procedures.

## Minor Gyms

Minor gyms are recognized local institutions outside the pillar tier.

**Why they exist:**
- Alternative badge pathways for challengers with different routes and timelines
- Type and training diversity not fully covered by pillar distribution
- Better regional coverage for smaller towns, routes, and local communities
- Teaching-first environments for foundational skill development
- Historical continuity for older or specialized gyms that no longer hold pillar designation

Minor gyms remain part of the broader trainer ecosystem, but they are generally less constrained by prestige management and mass challenger traffic.

## Scheduling and Access

Appointment pressure differs by tier.

**Typical wait windows:**
- Pillar gyms: usually 2-4 weeks, with longer waits during peak Conference season
- Minor gyms: usually a few days to about 1 week, with more flexible booking and occasional walk-in capacity

This split is primarily operational: pillar gyms process higher demand, while minor gyms absorb overflow and provide faster local access.

## Failed Attempt Cooldowns

Gyms apply cooldowns between failed attempts to protect safety, training quality, and staffing capacity. In practice, cooldown policies cluster into three tiers.

**Strict:**
- Typical interval: about 1 month
- Common rationale: traffic control, prestige standards, higher risk gating

**Moderate:**
- Typical interval: about 2 weeks
- Common rationale: balanced progression with time for targeted retraining

**Lenient:**
- Typical interval: about 1 week or no formal lockout
- Common rationale: instructional focus and rapid iteration for learners

Cooldown policy is not fully uniform across all gyms. Tier placement is usually tied to demand, leadership philosophy, and local safety norms.

## Battle Difficulty Model

Gym difficulty is not a single regional ladder. It is tiered by institutional model and local design.

**Pillar gyms:**
- Usually scale challenge intensity to challenger progression (often proxied by current badge count)
- May maintain multiple internal challenge bands to preserve fairness across novice and advanced entrants

**Minor gyms:**
- Often run fixed or lightly variable difficulty tracks
- Frequently emphasize instruction and accessible testing
- Some specialist minor gyms are intentionally high-difficulty despite non-pillar status

This creates a flexible route network: challengers can combine prestige-focused attempts with training-focused attempts instead of following one rigid sequence.

## Canon Divergence

**Official games ([Bulbapedia - Gym](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Gym), [Bulbapedia - Gym Leader](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Gym_Leader)):**
Most main-series regions present eight fixed gyms per region in a mostly linear progression model tied to story order.

**Pokemon Amber:**
The gym network is split into pillar and minor tiers, and badge routes are more flexible. Challenger order is less linear, and progression can mix prestige-focused and teaching-focused gym paths.

# International Licensing & Travel

This page defines how trainers legally move with their Pokemon between the "Big 4" regions in Amber continuity (Kanto–Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh). It favors practical interoperability over hard bans: no blanket prohibition on cross‑region parties; compliance hinges on registration, quarantine, and insurance.

## Kanto–Johto Exemption

Kanto and Johto operate under a **shared government** with sufficiently overlapping ecosystems that interregional carry restrictions do not apply between them. Trainers moving between Kanto and Johto may bring their full roster with no foreign Pokemon limits. Standard registration and health checks still apply at major crossings, but no visa tier or carry restriction is imposed.

All other Big 4 interregional travel (Kanto/Johto ↔ Hoenn, Hoenn ↔ Sinnoh, etc.) is subject to the full visa tier system.

## Core Framework

- International League Compact (ILC) — administrative agreement between the Big 4 League offices and their governments for:
  - Mutual recognition of trainer licenses (learner/standard/ACE)
  - Cross‑acceptance of gym badges for Junior League eligibility
  - Shared registries for Poké Ball serials and active party rosters
  - Incident data sharing (bans, suspensions, stolen Pokémon lists)

## Interregional Pokemon Carry — Visa Tiers

How many foreign Pokemon (species not native to the destination region) a trainer may carry is determined by their **visa tier**. This law is relatively recent — enacted roughly 15–25 years before story time as interregional travel expanded post-war and ecological damage from unchecked Pokemon movement became politically untenable. Older trainers remember when it didn’t exist.

The law is both **ecological** (invasive species prevention, habitat protection) and **political** (regional protectionism, local trainer economies).

### Visa Types & Foreign Pokemon Carry Limits

| Visa Type | Foreign Pokemon Allowed | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| **Tourist / Travel** | 1 | Companion only; no battling in gyms or official events |
| **Student** | 1–2 | Study-relevant species may require documentation |
| **Trainer** | 2–3 | Active registered team; gym battling permitted |
| **Researcher** | Up to 6 | Requires institutional sponsorship and documented purpose |
| **Official / Gym Leader / Government** | Unrestricted | Full diplomatic carry; highest scrutiny at customs |

Levels exist within each tier. A **provisional trainer visa** (new applicant, first interregional travel) allows 2 foreign Pokemon. A **senior trainer visa** (established record, multiple prior entries) allows 3. Upgrades require application, fee, and League endorsement.

### Practical Impact

- Most traveling trainers are on standard Trainer visas — they can bring 2–3 Pokemon from home and must fill the rest of their roster locally.
- Pokemon left behind stay at a licensed facility: home ranch (e.g., Oak’s lab), regional storage centers, or a trusted trainer’s care.
- This is why Ash Ketchum starts fresh each region — basic Trainer visa, 1–2 foreign Pokemon allowed, builds locally by necessity.
- For Amber: her roster management across regions is an active logistics operation. Oak’s ranch is not retirement — it’s the home base she rotates from.

### Exemptions & Edge Cases

- **Native species travelling with their trainer** do not count toward the foreign carry limit if they were caught in the destination region on a prior trip and registered there.
- **Eggs** are subject to separate import rules — some species flagged as invasive risk require permits even as eggs.
- **Rocket and poaching cases**: forged visas and smuggled Pokemon are a known problem; Rangers run spot checks at major ports.

## Bringing Pokemon Across Borders

- Registration: declare each active ball at exit and entry. Ball IDs and species link to the trainer’s license.
- Quarantine: only for flagged species (invasive pests, high‑risk pathogens) or if vaccination records are missing. Otherwise, routine inspection only.
- Vaccination/Health: Centers issue digital health stamps (core inoculations, recent treatment, parasite checks). Expired stamps trigger a quick clinic visit at customs.
- Carry Limits: active party cap (up to 6) honored across regions within visa allowances; additional storage permitted via licensed regional facilities.
- Prohibited Lists: narrow and ecological — certain plant/bug lines moved as eggs into protected biomes may require permits.

## Items & Tech

- Evolution Stones: legal to import/export; large shipments require customs declaration (commercial duty may apply).
- TMs/Disks: freely portable; some attack data tagged restricted for civil‑use (trainer must be 16+ or have pro permit).
- Ball Tech: serialized; spoofing equipment is contraband; Rangers and police run spot checks around ports.

## Insurance & Liability

- Minimum Insurance: inbound trainers must carry basic trainer liability (covers accidental damage during travel and quests). Sold at Centers; verificaton is part of customs.
- Quests: contracts remain region‑local; license must be recognized (ILC covers this) and insurance must name the local jurisdiction.

## League & Badges (Inter‑Region)

- Badges never expire and are cross‑recognized among the Big 4. A mixed set (e.g., 5 Kanto + 3 Hoenn) qualifies a junior for that year’s conference in their current region of residence.
- Seeding considers badge prestige/region but does not invalidate cross‑region collections.

## Enforcement & Security

- Ports of Entry: Indigo Plateau rail/air, Vermilion docks (to Hoenn), Olivine/Cianwood links, Slateport/Lilycove, Canalave/Snowpoint.
- MOUs: Rangers exchange alerts; shared watchlists for poachers, Rocket offshoots, and ball cloners.
- Trainer Obligations: obey local route closures, ball‑carry policies in cities, and spore/use‑of‑force rules. Violations can suspend ILC privileges.

## Narrative Notes (Amber)

- Visa tiers are the in-universe reason Amber rotates her roster between regions. She’s on a standard Trainer visa — 2–3 foreign Pokemon max. Oak’s ranch is her active logistics base, not a retirement home.
- The visa system also explains why Ash starts fresh every region canonically — basic Trainer visa, minimal foreign carry allowance, builds locally.
- Friction is bureaucratic (paperwork, inspections, visa upgrades), economic (tickets, insurance, storage fees), and safety (off-route risk) — not a hard legal wall, but a real constraint that shapes decisions.
- Conflicts arise when: paperwork lapses, a team includes a flagged species, visa tier doesn’t cover a planned team composition, or the trainer is under investigation (e.g., Rocket theft incidents).

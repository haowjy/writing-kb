# Quarterly Conference System (Big 4 Rotation)

## Overview

The quarterly conference system is the coordinated tournament calendar used across the Big 4 regions: Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, and Sinnoh. It functions similarly to tennis Grand Slams: four prestigious regional tournaments rotate across the year, elite competitors may pursue the full circuit, and most trainers only enter their home region's event.

## Big 4 Regions

The Big 4 are grouped as a single competitive cluster because they are geographically connected, have longstanding travel infrastructure, and maintain compatible League administration standards.

- **Regions in scope:** Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh
- **Shared framework:** Gym council governance compatibility, cross-region League administration, and badge recognition agreements
- **Practical connectivity:** Established ferry, rail, and flight routes between major ports and League hubs
- **Historical basis:** Post-war interregional cooperation that standardized tournament operations within this cluster

## Quarterly Rotation Schedule

Each region receives a three-month active season. The final month of each season hosts both a U19 event and an ACE-tier conference.

| Months | Active Region | Season-End Events |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-3 | Kanto | Kanto U19 Junior League Tournament; Kanto Conference (ACE tier) |
| 4-6 | Johto | Johto U19 Junior League Tournament; Johto Conference (ACE tier) |
| 7-9 | Hoenn | Hoenn U19 Junior League Tournament; Hoenn Conference (ACE tier) |
| 10-12 | Sinnoh | Sinnoh U19 Junior League Tournament; Sinnoh Conference (ACE tier) |

After Month 12, the cycle resets to Kanto.

## Competitive Tiers

### U19 Junior League

The U19 tier is the youth tournament layer.

- **Eligibility baseline:** Under 19, valid trainer license, and 8 badges from 8 different recognized gyms
- **Badge sourcing rule:** Badges may be mixed across Big 4 regions for qualification
- **Participation pattern:** Most youth entrants compete in one regional event per year because travel and logistics are limiting factors

### Conference Tier (ACE Level)

The conference tier is the elite competitive layer.

- **Eligibility baseline:** ACE-level capability and League recognition beyond standard junior qualification
- **Role in progression:** Primary high-tier conference path tied to Elite Four and champion-track competition pipelines
- **Participation pattern:** Many ACE trainers run the full annual circuit (Kanto -> Johto -> Hoenn -> Sinnoh)

## Battle Formats & Roster Rules

Tournaments use a **registration pool** system — trainers register more Pokemon than they field, then select a subset for each round.

| Format | Register | Field per round |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Singles | 10 | 6 |
| Doubles | 10 | 6 (2 active at a time) |
| Tag Team | 10 | 3 |

- Registered pool is locked at the start of the tournament
- Field selection is made fresh each round — trainers adapt to their matchup
- This creates strategic depth: bench Pokemon like a specialist with a niche ability can be pulled for the right opponent

## Cross-Region Badge Validity

Badge eligibility is interoperable across the Big 4.

- A mixed badge set is valid for U19 entry if the trainer has 8 distinct gym badges
- Region of issue does not invalidate badge count for tournament registration
- Cross-recognition supports interregional travel and avoids duplicate badge-grinding for each host region

## Gradual World Opening Model

At present, the coordinated circuit applies to the Big 4 only. Other regions are treated as gradually opening frontiers rather than fully integrated conference hosts.

- **Currently accessible core:** Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh
- **Later integration model:** Additional regions open over decades as routes are surveyed, cleared, and stabilized
- **Operational driver:** ACE trainers and Ranger-aligned operations perform high-risk route clearing, mapping, and maintenance work needed for safe long-distance interregional travel
- **System effect:** Competitive globalization is paced by infrastructure and safety capacity, not by a single political declaration

## Canon Divergence

**Official Pokemon canon:** Regional leagues are primarily presented as region-local structures with their own qualification flows; official material does not define a unified Big 4 quarterly rotation calendar.

**Pokemon Amber:** Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, and Sinnoh are treated as a coordinated annual circuit with fixed quarterly handoffs and interoperable badge qualification across the four-region cluster.

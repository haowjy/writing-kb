# Career Paths & Type-as-Profession

How trainer success translates into career outcomes, and how type specialization functions as a profession in this world.

## Tournament Structure

**Age Brackets:**
- **Under-19 Junior League** (high school age: 14-18 years old; requires 8 gym badges OR school graduation OR gym leader recommendation)
- Under-25 Challenger League
- Open League (all ages)
- Master League (invitation only)

**Typical Badge Outcomes:**
- **2-3 badges:** Respectable, demonstrates work ethic, employable
- **4-6 badges:** Very successful, qualifies for specialist positions
- **7-8 badges:** Elite achievement, leadership positions
- **Conference qualification:** Top 1%, exceptional

## Career Impact

**Badges as Resume:**
- More badges = better career opportunities
- Team composition suggests industry specialization
- Tournament placements show competitive ability
- Years training shows commitment

## Type Specialization as Profession

Choosing a type specialty is culturally almost like choosing a profession --- families in certain industries pass down type specializations across generations:

**Ground/Rock types** → Mining, geology, construction industries
- Example: Mining families with multi-generational Ground-type tradition
- Family businesses expect children to train Ground-types for future work
- Type choice = career preparation, not just battle preference

**Water types** → Fishing, maritime shipping, water treatment facilities
- Coastal families traditionally raise Water-type Pokemon
- Industry needs Pokemon-assisted labor (transportation, rescue, management)

**Fire types** → Metalworking, forges, energy plants, industrial manufacturing
- High-temperature industries require Fire-type Pokemon
- Apprenticeships passed down with Fire-type teams

**Electric types** → Power plants, technology sectors, communications
- Modern industries driving Electric-type specialization growth

**Psychic types** → Research institutions, medicine, education, government
- Elite professions correlate with Psychic-type specialization
- Silph Co. and tech companies favor Psychic specialists

**Fighting types** → Security services, military, construction, physical labor
- Traditional warrior clans (like Kyle's Fighting Dojo family)
- Manual labor industries benefit from Fighting-type strength

**Grass types** → Agriculture, forestry, environmental management
- Farming families pass down Grass-type knowledge

**Generalist teams** → Management, coordination, teaching, Rangers
- Versatility valued in leadership positions

**Cultural Reality:**
- Not absolute (people can choose different types)
- But strong correlation and family expectation
- "Following the family tradition" = choosing parents' type specialty
- Breaking tradition (choosing different type) = statement of independence
- Career opportunities often favor matching type specialization

## Social Stratification

**Trainer Tiers:**
1. Elite Trainers (Champions, Elite Four, top Gym Leaders)
2. Professional Trainers (6-8 badges, strong teams, career established)
3. Competent Trainers (3-5 badges, decent teams, stable work)
4. Casual Trainers (0-2 badges, one companion, normal life)
5. Non-Trainers (career without Pokemon - rare in this world)

**Social Mobility:**
- Skill can elevate poor trainers (merit-based)
- But resources make success much easier
- Natural talent + hard work can overcome class
- Wealth advantages persist (bred Pokemon, funding, equipment)

# Licensing System & Trainer Tiers

How trainer credentials, carry limits, and recognized tiers work. The licensing system is decentralized --- gyms (as regional authorities) gate the major progression milestones.

## One Pokemon = Constitutional Right

**No License Required:**
- Every person legally allowed 1 Pokemon without paperwork
- Foundational right in Pokemon society
- Can travel, battle casually with one companion
- Often family Pokemon, childhood gift, or companion
- **Cannot catch additional Pokemon** without Trainer License

**Who Has Just One:**
- Regular citizens with companion Pokemon
- Children not yet pursuing training career
- Those who want companionship without trainer path
- People fleeing circumstances (legal to have one without paperwork)

## Trainer ID: Easy Registration

**Purpose:**
- Legal recognition as trainer
- Access to quest boards
- Required to challenge gyms officially
- Pokemon Center formal registration

**Requirements:**
- Must already possess at least 1 Pokemon
- Proof of identity (loosely enforced in decentralized system)
- Basic literacy (read signs, fill forms)
- Pass basic competency test:
  - Demonstrate Pokemon listens to you (sit, stay, come, use move, stop)
  - Safety knowledge questions
  - 15-30 minutes, administered by existing trainer at Pokemon Center
  - ₽5-10 fee, almost everyone who tries passes

**No Age Restriction:**
- Anyone can get Trainer ID at any age
- Guardian presence encouraged for young children but not required
- Most people wait until ready for journey/school (14+)

**Does NOT Unlock:**
- Multi-Pokemon carry (still limited to 1 until license obtained)

## Trainer Tiers

Trainer progression moves through recognized tiers. Each tier unlocks different carry limits, quest access, gym rights, visa eligibility, and field authority.

| Tier | Requirement | Carry | Quests | Visa (Interregional) | Field Authority |
|------|-------------|-------|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Novice** | Trainer ID only | 1 | Class D | Tourist (1 foreign) | None |
| **Licensed Trainer** | Trainer License | 3 | Class D--C | Provisional Trainer (1 foreign) | Basic |
| **Trainer** | License + 1--3 badges | 3--4 | Class C--B | Standard Trainer (2 foreign) | Basic |
| **Senior Trainer** | 4--7 badges | 4--5 | Class B--A | Senior Trainer (3 foreign) | Moderate |
| **Veteran Trainer** | 8 badges, conference qualified | 6 | Class A | Senior Trainer (3 foreign) | Moderate--High |
| **ACE Trainer** | ≥1 Elite Badge; government-contracted | 6 | Class A--S | Official/Government (high scrutiny, diplomatic protocols, carry unrestricted but heavily documented) | High --- regional asset, emergency response |
| **Elite / Champion-track** | Elite Four pipeline | 6 | Unrestricted | Official-tier (unrestricted) | Full |

**Notes:**
- Kanto and Johto are exempt from interregional carry rules --- shared government, overlapping ecosystems, no border restrictions between them.
- ACE Trainers are government-contracted regional assets --- practically the region's military. Interregional travel involves diplomatic protocols and heavy documentation, not a free pass. They can carry more but with more oversight.
- ACE status requires ≥1 Elite Badge (elite gym challenge); not automatically granted by badge count alone.
- Field authority includes: filing Ranger requests, leading rescue missions, independent route operations, Ranger cooperation on incidents.
- Most trainers never exceed Senior Trainer tier; most stop at 2--4 badges.

## Graduated Carry Limits

Carry limits scale with demonstrated competency. The license is the floor; badges expand it incrementally.

| Milestone | Carry Limit |
|-----------|-------------|
| Trainer ID only | 1 Pokemon |
| **Trainer License** | **3 Pokemon** |
| 2 Gym Badges | 4 Pokemon |
| 4 Gym Badges | 5 Pokemon |
| 6 Gym Badges | 6 Pokemon |

**Trainer License:**
- Obtained separately from Trainer ID --- requires passing a more substantive competency evaluation
- Unlocks carry limit of 3; this is the first real team-building threshold
- Most journeying trainers get their license before or shortly after their first badge

**Why Badges, Not Exams:**
- Gyms ARE regional authority (decentralized government)
- Gym leaders best positioned to evaluate real competency
- Personal judgment more reliable than standardized test
- Prevents mass-catching without proven skill (Mt. Silver lesson)
- Forces engagement with gym system

**Practical Impact:**
- Can get Trainer ID easily at 14+ (or any age) but can only carry 1
- License unlocks 3 --- the first real team
- Each carry expansion requires meaningful badge progress
- Full 6-Pokemon team not possible until 6 badges earned
- Most trainers never reach 6 (most stop at 2-4 badges)

**Carry Limit vs. Total Ownership:**
- Carry limit applies to active Pokemon on your person (in balls, out of balls)
- Trainers can own more Pokemon than their carry limit --- excess live at ranches, stables, Oak's lab, or trusted facilities
- No centralized PC storage network exists at this point in the timeline --- "boxing" a Pokemon means physically leaving it somewhere with someone
- Stored/stabled Pokemon don't count toward carry limit (not actively bonded)

**Carry Limit (6 Pokemon):**
- Legal regulation AND physiological safety threshold
- Exceeding 6 = risk of cumulative backlash if Pokemon faint
- Over-carry exceptions: facility drills, Ranger crisis waivers, elite Ace trainers

# Beatriz Romero

Beatriz Romero is one of [[alonso-quijano]]'s Paldean adventurer-crew companions. She is quiet around people, intense around Pokemon, and driven by a huge field-naturalist dream:

> **To discover and catalog every Pokemon.**

She is part of the small Paldean crew that traveled to Kanto with Alonso. During the Route 1 / Viridian off-route incident, Alonso is separated from the crew after chasing trouble/getting lost; Beatriz and the others are likely the people Charcadet, Ditto, and Teddiursa can lead back to rescue Amber and Alonso after the cave collapse.

## Core Drive

Beatriz's obsession is knowledge through direct observation. She wants the world written down correctly: species, habitats, behavior, diet, social patterns, moves, migration, warning signs, and the differences between rumor and reality.

This is not fame-first. Her childhood taught her that people hurt Pokemon when they misunderstand them.

## Childhood

Beatriz grew up in rural/coastal/mountain-edge Paldea around ordinary working Pokemon rather than elite battle culture. As a child, she noticed adults constantly got Pokemon wrong:

- calling Pokemon pests when they were reacting to nesting pressure,
- treating rare Pokemon as rumors or trophies,
- repeating folk explanations that did not match what she observed,
- blaming attacks on aggression when the behavior had a pattern,
- ignoring habitat signs until conflict happened.

She became the child with notebooks.

At first, she wrote things down because the adults were wrong. Over time, that habit became a life mission: if nobody records Pokemon accurately, people keep hurting them for the wrong reasons.

## Personality

- Quiet around people; much more animated around Pokemon.
- Carries notebooks/sketches/field tools.
- Corrects species facts under her breath, then more firmly if ignored.
- Cares about accuracy more than politeness.
- Notices small ecological details before social ones.
- Can look passive until someone endangers a Pokemon or falsifies a record.
- Often the crew member who remembers supplies, conditions, weather, and what Alonso forgot.

## Relationship to Alonso

Alonso wants to become the **Greatest Adventurer**. Beatriz understands the appeal, but her version of adventure is not glory; it is finding and understanding Pokemon no one has properly documented.

She follows Alonso because he reaches impossible places. She stays with him because his courage opens doors her notebooks alone cannot.

Tension:

- Alonso asks, "Who is brave enough to go?"
- Beatriz asks, "Who is careful enough to understand what they find?"

She can admire Alonso while also thinking he is an idiot who should have packed more rope.

## Relationship to Ciro

[[ciro-salvatierra]] treats discoveries as potential income. Beatriz treats them as knowledge first.

This creates constant friction:

- Ciro wants to sell maps, leads, guide services, or information.
- Beatriz wants accurate records and protection from careless exploitation.
- Ciro may joke about monetizing her notes; Beatriz may threaten to break his fingers if he falsifies a habitat report.

They are not enemies. Ciro's lines matter: he will scheme for profit, but he will not abandon someone in danger.

## Relationship to Amber

If Beatriz meets Amber during the Route 1 rescue aftermath, she should register Amber less as a dramatic hero and more as an anomaly in the field record:

- a very young Kanto trainer,
- traveling with an unusual Ditto,
- involved with Teddiursa/Ursaring behavior under Rocket interference,
- observed by Charcadet,
- weirdly competent for her age.

Beatriz may become one of the first people to quietly notice that Amber's situation does not fit normal trainer categories, though she does not need to know why.

## Story Function

Beatriz expands adventuring beyond Alonso's greatness myth. She shows adventure as cataloging, field science, and responsibility to the Pokemon being discovered.

She also mirrors young [[professor-oak]]: the dream of seeing and understanding every Pokemon, before age and history transform that fire into institutional research.

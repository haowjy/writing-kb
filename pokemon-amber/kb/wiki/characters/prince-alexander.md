# Prince Alexander

Escaped prince of **Argaea** (the hidden kingdom), and the saga's study of **liberation that does not know how to stop**. His name follows the "A" theme (Amber / Alonso / Alexander) and deliberately invokes **Alexander the Great**: the king who set out to free and ended up conquering. His three-act shape across the saga is rescued -> restored -> conqueror.

> [!NOTE] **Kingdom name: Argaea** (decided). From the **Argead** dynasty, Macedon's royal house --- a *concealed* play, chosen so the conquest twist is **not** telegraphed to most readers. (Plain `Macedon` would have foreshadowed it loudly; `Argaea` conceals it.)

---

## What Is Established

- **Chronologically ~3,000 years old, biologically a youth** --- Argaea fell into *stasis* when it was folded into the Mega Dimension, so Alexander is the literal ancient prince skipped forward, not a descendant.
- **The escape:** his parents' two legendaries --- **Cresselia** (the King's partner) and **Shaymin** (the Queen's) --- broke him out of the dream and escaped *with* him. The **King and Queen stayed trapped**; only Alexander made it out. He travels with both legendaries as his parents' entrusted guardians. See `wiki/arcs/arc-hidden-kingdom.md` (Origin).
- **Parents:** the **King (his father) becomes the Mad King** Amber defeats in Arc 10 --- hollowed because he gave Cresselia away and held the nightmare alone. The **Queen (Shaymin's keeper)** is trapped with him.
- Met early (Arc 1) alongside Alonso; effectively the first contact between sealed Argaea and the outside world in ~3,000 years. His emergence is a **symptom of the failing seal**, not a planned mission.
- **Arc 16:** returns with Cresselia + Shaymin to purify/reseal Mega Darkrai, then **reclaims the throne**.
- **Endgame:** becomes a conqueror after Amber invites his kingdom's army to liberate Kanto (see below).

## Role in Story

### Arc 1 --- First Contact

Alexander surfaces in the outside world alongside Alonso, encountered by Amber early. He is a prince with no map for the world he's fallen into: a civilization frozen for three millennia meeting the Mega era. He arrives **with Cresselia and Shaymin** --- his parents' partners, who carried him out of the fold. **No tutor** (that idea was cut).

> [!FLAG] His specific Arc 1 role, what he's fleeing, how much he understands about the seal, and his early relationship to Amber and Alonso are open.

### Arc 16 --- The Restoration

After Amber's trusted raid team purifies and reseals Mega Darkrai and frees Gardevoir, Alexander **reclaims the throne**. This is the rightful-king beat: legitimacy restored through sacrifice, set against the saga's later motif of Giovanni's **captured / counterfeit legitimacy** (puppet champions). At this stage he is the sympathetic answer to Giovanni --- restored sovereignty vs seized power. The turn is what complicates that.

### Endgame --- The Liberator Who Doesn't Stop

When Giovanni's pawn seizes Kanto, **Amber asks Alexander to invade and free it from Team Rocket.** He agrees. His kingdom's army takes Kanto --- and then **does not leave. Liberation curdles into conquest.** The two-body endgame (Giovanni vs coalition) becomes a three-body problem, and the third conqueror is the one Amber invited in herself.

This is, structurally, **Amber's error**: under desperation she reaches for concentrated foreign force to solve Kanto fast, abandoning her own model of distributed, voluntary trust. The consequences are the cost of that choice.

> [!FLAG] **Proposed, not locked.** Whether Alexander is a *mid-endgame complication* that resolves before the Giovanni summit, or a *co-final threat*, is undecided. The locked spine keeps **Amber vs Giovanni** as the culmination; a second conqueror must not eclipse it. Resolution should come **from within** (his own people / the line he won't admit he crossed), not by Amber assembling a bigger army --- that would relaunch the exact force-logic the endgame exists to reject.

## The Conquest Engine

Why Alexander can't stop --- grounded in the **real** Alexander the Great, two phases:

**Phase 1 --- Necessity (he genuinely "has to").**
The historical Alexander inherited a near-empty treasury, ~500 talents of his father's debt, and took on ~800 more in loans to launch the Persian campaign (Arrian, Opis speech). He had a large professional army he *had* to keep paying; the conquest **had to self-fund**, and stopping early meant bankruptcy and mutiny. Mirror: the hidden kingdom mobilizes at ruinous cost to march into the modern world at Amber's request. To not collapse, it must take Kanto's resources. Early on, Alexander is **right** --- he has no off-ramp that isn't ruin.

**Phase 2 --- Momentum (the trap springs).**
Once the Persian treasuries fell, the historical Alexander was rich --- and **kept marching anyway**, into Bactria and India, on *pothos*: ambition, the army's own logic, the self-image of the hero-king. Mirror: once the spoils come in, the kingdom's survival no longer requires conquest --- but the marching doesn't stop. **This is the exact moment liberation becomes conquest: when the justification runs out but the momentum doesn't.**

The historical off-ramp is the tell for the fictional one: Alexander was finally halted **by his own soldiers' refusal** at the Hyphasis (326 BC), not by an enemy.

This keeps Alexander **tragic, not villainous** --- trapped by a machine he started in good faith --- and it makes Amber complicit in the mechanism, not just unlucky.

> [!FLAG] The *human texture* of Phase 2 is open: does Alexander hate what he's doing, believe it's still liberation, or go cold and certain? (See tone fork below.)

## No Mentor *(cut)*

An earlier idea gave Alexander an accompanying tutor/manservant (an Aristotle-to-Alexander figure, in several possible roles). **Cut** --- the author decided against it. His companions in exile are instead his parents' two legendaries, **Cresselia and Shaymin** (see Origin / Relationships).

## Core Philosophy: Drive on the Conquest Axis

Alexander belongs on the saga's [[../themes/drive-spectrum|drive / obsession spectrum]], occupying a slot none of the others do: **ambition you cannot put down even when you want to.** Where Giovanni *chooses* conquest from conviction and Amber *refuses to stop* to stay un-vulnerable, Alexander **can't stop because the machine he started won't let him** --- drive as entrapment rather than appetite.

He also sharpens the saga's legitimacy theme by contrast:

- **Giovanni:** seized / counterfeit legitimacy (puppet champions, captured institutions).
- **Alexander:** *restored, rightful* legitimacy --- which makes his conquest worse, not better. A just king is the hardest conqueror to argue against, especially if his rule is genuinely better than the Rocket-rotted institutions he replaced.

The cruelest version: **Alexander is not wrong on the merits**, and Amber has to oppose a liberator whose results are good and whose claim is legitimate.

> [!FLAG] **Tone fork (open):** is Phase-2 Alexander *sincerely blind* (still believes he's liberating), *righteously certain* (knows and is at peace with it), or *something colder*? This is the single biggest undecided lever on his character.

## Relationships

- **Amber:** she frees his people and is the reason he can reclaim his throne (reciprocal trust --- her model). She is also the one who invites his army into Kanto, making their endgame collision personal: the friend who restored him and the friend who unleashed him.
- **Alonso:** met alongside in Arc 1. Both are figures of chosen greatness; Alonso is the version that *stays* generous, Alexander the version that curdles --- a deliberate pairing.
- **His parents (the King & Queen):** bonded to Cresselia and Shaymin; both trapped in Argaea. He carries their partners --- and, in Arc 16, returns to free the kingdom they died holding.
- **Cresselia & Shaymin:** his parents' legendaries, his guardians in exile, and the mechanism of Arc 16's purification. Two healing legendaries that also seed his endgame strategic-force turn.

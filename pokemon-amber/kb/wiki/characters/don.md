# Don

A **Paldean adventuring knight**, age 19–20, who traveled from Paldea to Kanto as part of an expedition crew before striking out on his own. An outsider who chose to come here, not born into Kanto's systems. He is the real deal — exceptional, sincere, competent — a knight who actually means it.

## Role in Story

### Arc 1: Early Mentor

Don is Amber's temporary early mentor during Arc 1. His mentorship is **existential, not tactical** — he teaches through example and presence, not skill drills or technique lectures. Amber learns from watching someone who genuinely embodies the ideals she's inherited as intellectual concepts (game knowledge).

### Endgame Return

Don returns as a **peak-level ally** in the saga's endgame. At that point, he has grown to peak trainer level alongside Amber's development. He fights as an equal, not a mentor — the former teacher and student standing together.

## Personality

Don is **special** — not in the "chosen one" sense, but in the sense that he's genuinely exceptional and sincere and competent. He is:

- **Earnest without being foolish** — he believes in knightly ideals and lives them, but he's not naive about the world
- **Not cynical** — he hasn't been broken or corroded by experience; he chose his ideals and they've held
- **A knight who actually means it** — the honor, the worthiness, the discipline are not performance or armor against the world; they're what he is

He walks into Kanto with a framework that doesn't map cleanly onto the trainer/gym/badge system, and he doesn't try to force the fit. He simply is what he is, and people who spend time around him recognize it.

## Core Philosophy: Obsession, Drive, Ambition

Don represents the **"chosen ideal"** variant of the saga's [[../themes/core-story-elements|obsession theme]]. Multiple characters in this story want things intensely — Giovanni wants Kanto supremacy, Fuji wants his daughter back, Amber wants to survive and find her place. Don's version is the cleanest:

> He wants to become great. Genuinely great. And he pursues that with sincerity and discipline.

His ambition is **not moralized** as good or bad — it simply IS what he is and what he does. He is the exemplar of "wanting to be great" without corruption, without shortcuts, without the rot that consumes other driven characters in the saga. The discipline is the point. The pursuit is the life.

This anchors the obsession theme by showing what it looks like when it's healthy — a counterpoint to Giovanni's corrupted drive and the Rocket ideology.

## The Charcadet Connection

Don is **why Charcadet is in Kanto.**

He brought Charcadet from Paldea as part of his early team. Charcadet was with him through the expedition crossing — a companion from home, not a capture. When Don encounters Amber in Arc 1 and sees the beginning of her quest, Charcadet observes her too.

Eventually **Charcadet chooses Amber.**

Don letting Charcadet go is not a failure or a loss — it's the proof of his ideals. A knight who genuinely believes in worthiness over possession. The principle is:

> **Don is why Charcadet is in Kanto. Amber is why Charcadet stays.**

This is the origin of the psychic stone that Charcadet carries out of the Day 2 cave (see [[../arcs/saga-overview]]), which eventually becomes Mewtwonite Y — a chain of events that connects Don's Paldean expedition to the saga's endgame.

- Armarouge (ace)

## Paldea vs. Kanto Myths

Don imports the **Paldean adventurer myth** into Kanto — a structural contrast that matters for the story:

| Kanto Myth | Paldea Myth |
|---|---|
| Trainer / journey / badges / League | Adventurer / Area Zero / expeditions / Treasure Hunt |
| Competitive progression (8 badges → conference) | Exploratory progression (what did you find? what did you survive?) |
| Institutional (Gyms, League, government) | Personal (knightly Charcadet line, earned worthiness) |
| Standardized paths | Forged paths |

Don admires adventuring knights broadly. The Horizons Explorers are one example he respects, but not the only one — he's not a fanboy, just someone who recognizes kindred spirits.

When Don enters Kanto, he doesn't reject the gym system — he simply operates in a frame where a gym badge is one marker among many, not the organizing principle of a trainer's life. This gives Amber a different lens on her own journey, even before she fully internalizes it.

## Interregional Travel Context

Don's arrival in Kanto is **plausible but not casual:**

- He came as part of a **Paldean adventurer crew / fellowship / expedition** — organized crossing, not solo
- Once in Kanto, he broke off on a **reckless solo quest** — the expedition was the gateway, not the end
- Interregional travel in this world is **possible but not normalized** — dangerous wilderness between regions, though Repel, Poke Balls, healing infrastructure, ships, aviation, communications, and mapping are gradually making it more viable
- Don is an early adopter of interregional movement — he's there before the infrastructure is easy, which says something about him

See [[../lore/trainer-systems/index]] for the trainer systems context; Don exists partially outside those systems as a foreign adventurer.

## Story Role Summary

### Current (Arc 1)
- Encountered early in Kanto, during Amber's badge push
- Existential mentor: presence and example, not tactical instruction
- Charcadet observes Amber through their shared time; the choice to let Charcadet go defines Don's character
- Departs Kanto after Arc 1 — his own journey continues elsewhere

### Future (Endgame)
- Returns at peak trainer level — equal, not mentor
- Fights alongside Amber against the saga's final threats (Team Rocket dismantling, legendary crises)
- The Charcadet connection hangs between them — Don let go, Amber lost; both carry that

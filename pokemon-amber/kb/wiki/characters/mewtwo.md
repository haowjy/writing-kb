# Mewtwo

Created by Dr. Fuji under Team Rocket's Project Chimera — a clone of Mew, weaponized. The "successful" product of the same lab that produced Ditto and Amber.

## Origin

Fuji's primary deliverable to Team Rocket: clone Mew into a controllable weapon. Mewtwo is the result. For Fuji personally, Mewtwo was the stepping stone — the proof of concept that would let him perfect the techniques needed to resurrect Amber. Mewtwo was never the goal. Amber was.

Mewtwo carries human DNA admixture as part of the creation process. This is why it holds grudges, processes betrayal the way a person does rather than the way a Pokemon does, and hesitates around "Amber" rather than attacking.

## Connection to Amber

Both are Fuji's creations. Mewtwo from Mew directly; Amber from a human clone with trace Mew DNA. They share an origin in the same lab, the same scientist, the same source material at a remove.

Mewtwo's hesitation to kill the scientists at the moment of awakening is attributed to the familiar presence of "Amber" — the clone he had been exposed to in the lab. Whether that recognition extends to the current Amber (an isekai'd soul in that body, not Fuji's daughter) is an open question.

## Story Role

Off-screen variable for most of the saga. Contact events should be rare and consequential — not a recurring presence, but a region-scale force that exists in the background.

The full Mewtwo thread — its relationship to Amber, Ditto, Fuji's legacy, and the Mew connection — is the spine of the saga. Needs its own planning session.

## Known Facts

- Holds grudges; interprets threats through a human cognitive frame
- Recognizes "Amber" — exhibits protective/curious behavior rather than aggression
- Escaped the Cinnabar lab at the moment of awakening (ch1)
- Whereabouts during Amber's journey: unknown

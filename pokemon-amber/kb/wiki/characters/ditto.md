# Ditto

Amber's first Pokemon. Originally Dr. Fuji's ace — a trained weapon. Now something else entirely.

## Origin

Ditto aren't a natural species. They're a byproduct of genetic cloning research — Fuji pioneered it, others followed, and every Ditto in the world traces back to that kind of mistake. A cloning attempt goes wrong, produces a blob that can temporarily become the source Pokemon but always imperfectly, and a Ditto exists. They escaped labs, bred, spread. The species is entirely man-made.

The universal defect: every Ditto keeps its own face. Wrong eyes, wrong mouth, Ditto's expression bleeding through whatever form it's wearing. That flaw is baked into all of them — it's not a quirk of any one specimen, it's the consistent failure mode of the cloning process itself.

This Ditto is different. It is a failed **Mew** clone.

No face bleed-through. Full morphological fidelity. And when it transforms, it doesn't get a weakened copy of the form — it gets the strength too. Both of these things were thought to be impossible for a Ditto. Oak says it out loud in ch16: *"Even its strength is copied? I thought Ditto transformations were always incomplete..."*

Mew is the origin of all Pokemon genetics and natively knows Transform — not as a learned move but as something fundamental to what it is. A clone of Mew carries that. The result is a Ditto that transforms the way Mew does: completely. And crucially, it can *remember* transformations. Practice them. Lock them in permanently. Standard Ditto cannot do this.

## What Fuji Made It

Fuji recognized what he had and trained it systematically. Drilled it on specific high-value forms until they were remembered:

- Gyarados
- Nidoking
- Rhydon
- Gengar
- Arcanine
- Ninetales

Essentially Giovanni's heaviest hitters and Blaine's ace — the strongest Pokemon Fuji had studied or had access to through Team Rocket. "Protocol Five" and similar numbered commands trigger specific transformations instantly.

This made Ditto Fuji's personal ace. A shapeshifting weapon with a memorized repertoire, capable of fielding forms at full strength.

Professor Oak's surprise in ch16 — *"Even its strength is copied? I thought Ditto transformations were always incomplete..."* — reflects this. For normal Ditto, the transformations are always incomplete. This one is exceptional, and Oak doesn't know why.

## What It Actually Is

Underneath the training: a Mew-descended Pokemon. Mew is curious, playful, life-affirming. That's in there.

Fuji built the weapon. He never particularly tried to break what was underneath.

## The Killing Arc (ch13)

Fuji had used Ditto to fight before. He had never ordered it to kill.

Ch13 is the first time. The sequence:

1. **Dragon Rage on Poliwag** — complies fully
2. **Hyper Beam on Stephen** — visible resistance (coils back, eyes meet Stephen's, scales trembling), then fires — but deliberately weakened. Fuji's immediate response: *"Why did you hold back?"* Stephen is crumpled, not gone.
3. **"Finish them. Bite."** — refuses. Form begins dissolving.
4. Fuji recalls it as useless. Sends Magmar instead. Magmar kills without hesitation.

Ditto tried to thread the needle — fire but not kill — and it didn't work. Stephen still dies. The refusal wasn't enough to save him.

By ch15-16, ordered to kill the children and then Oak, it refuses from the start. The arc is the gradual collapse of its compliance: it can fight, it will fight, but somewhere in the Mew DNA there is a floor it cannot cross.

## With Amber

Amber didn't try to bond with Ditto. She was just living in Fuji's apartment, scared, figuring things out — and Ditto was there. That was enough.

Mew is playful. Being around someone who wasn't performing anything for it, who was genuinely present, was probably novel. The bond formed quietly and without ceremony.

By the time of ch15-16, the orders Fuji gives involve Amber directly. That's where the compliance ends entirely.

## After (Oak → Amber)

At the end of ch16, Oak catches Ditto from the scorched yard. Ditto goes in the ball without resistance. Oak gives it to Amber at the end of ch17, before her licensed departure.

Whether Oak tells Amber what Ditto is — a failed Mew clone, exceptional, trained as a weapon — is TBD. She may receive it knowing only that it's Fuji's Ditto that refused to obey him.

## Capabilities

- Transforms into any Pokemon with near-perfect morphological fidelity
- Retains close to full strength in transformed forms (not the typical weakened Ditto copy)
- Can remember and hold specific transformations without a live model present — currently locked in: Gyarados, Nidoking, Rhydon, Gengar, Arcanine, Ninetales
- Cannot copy non-physical attributes: memories, learned strategies, complex technique — only form and biomechanics

**What it is under Amber:** The remembered repertoire is Fuji's. What forms it learns going forward will be Amber's.

# Ciro Salvatierra

Ciro Salvatierra is one of [[alonso-quijano]]'s Paldean adventurer-crew companions: charming, semi-shady, money-minded, daring, and more noble under pressure than he wants people to notice.

His surface dream is blunt:

> **To get rich.**

His deeper motive is independence. If he makes his own fortune, no family, creditor, employer, patron, or empty stomach gets to decide what his life is for.

## Core Drive

Ciro's obsession is money as freedom and proof.

He likes wealth, comfort, status, and winning deals. He also wants the security of knowing nobody can buy him, hire him cheap, patronize him, trap him in debt, or use necessity as a leash.

This should be blunt and material: Ciro grew up dirt poor, scrounging for scraps, always aware of who had food, who had gear, and who could afford mistakes. His background can be either orphan-adjacent or dysfunctional-family poverty, but the stronger current version is a family that constantly fell into debt because the adults wasted money on frivolous things while Ciro learned to survive the consequences.

## Childhood / Dirt-Poor Scrounger Background

Ciro is **not** from minor nobility. He grew up dirt poor and learned to scrounge for scraps. The current preferred version is dysfunctional-family poverty rather than clean orphan tragedy: adults around him had a horrible habit of spending scarce money on frivolous things, chasing little luxuries or bad bets, then leaving the household in debt again.

His childhood pressures were simple and humiliating:

- find food before pride,
- grab loose change before someone else does,
- patch gear instead of replacing it,
- dodge creditors and angry shopkeepers,
- never trust an adult's promise that money will be fine tomorrow,
- if you cannot pay, someone else decides for you.

An orphan version remains possible if needed later, but the core is the same: he grew up without financial safety. Money was not abstract status; it was whether he ate, whether he could travel, whether someone could corner him, and whether he had to beg.

He learned early that poverty makes ownership indirect. People do not need to literally own you if they own your food, gear, debt, route access, or next favor.

His response was not solemn rebellion. It was hustling.

## Personality

- Charming, quick-talking, and shameless about opportunities.
- Always looking for profit, leverage, resale value, sponsorship, or a loophole.
- Semi-shady but not malicious: sells maps, guide services, information, introductions, and possibly overpriced convenience.
- Dresses upmarket when useful, scoundrel when useful, helpful when unavoidable.
- Loves risk if the reward is real.
- Complains when heroism is unpaid, then does it anyway.

## Code

Ciro is not a thief as his core identity and should not read as evil. He is a hustler with lines.

He may:

- overcharge a rich trainer,
- sell a questionable map,
- bluff officials,
- exploit loopholes,
- negotiate too aggressively,
- monetize discoveries faster than Beatriz likes.

He will not:

- abandon someone helpless,
- sell out a companion,
- hand Pokemon to abusers,
- back down from a fight when someone vulnerable is in danger.

His nobility is not etiquette. It is the line he refuses to cross.

## Relationship to Alonso

Alonso wants to become the **Greatest Adventurer**. Ciro thinks that sounds ridiculous and also keeps following him.

Their clash:

- Alonso: "Someone has to."
- Ciro: "Someone should pay."

But when danger arrives, Ciro's actions reveal the shared core: he will protect people even when there is no profit in it. Alonso gives Ciro's daring a nobler shape; Ciro keeps Alonso from pretending logistics and money do not matter.

## Relationship to Beatriz

[[beatriz-romero]] wants to discover and catalog every Pokemon. Ciro wants to make discoveries pay.

Their friction is productive:

- Beatriz cares about accuracy; Ciro cares about value.
- Beatriz distrusts exploitation; Ciro distrusts unpaid labor.
- Beatriz writes the field notes; Ciro asks who is buying the guidebook.

He should annoy her constantly, but he also respects her expertise because bad information gets people killed and ruins future profit.

## Relationship to Amber

If Ciro meets Amber during the Route 1 rescue aftermath, he should probably clock the practical situation faster than the emotional one:

- Oak-backed young trainer,
- unusual Ditto,
- Paldean Charcadet paying attention to her,
- Rocket activity nearby,
- possible compensation/reward/liability questions.

He may joke about billing Alonso for rescue costs. But if Amber is hurt, he helps.

## Pokemon / Team Direction

Undecided. His team should support mobility, negotiation, opportunism, and dirty-but-not-evil tactics rather than pure villainy.

Possible roles:

- fast scout,
- lock/utility partner,
- flashy status-symbol Pokemon,
- partner that helps with trade, transport, or staged distractions.

Avoid giving him a Team Rocket-coded team unless the goal is deliberate misread.

## Story Function

Ciro expands adventuring beyond Alonso's nobility and Beatriz's field science. He shows adventure as money, freedom, hustle, and risk.

He also helps define Alonso by contrast: Alonso does not care enough about profit; Ciro cares too much until someone needs help.

# Celadon Gym

**Celadon Gym** is one of the eight traditional Kanto Pokemon Gyms, specializing in Grass-type Pokemon. Located in [Celadon City](./index.md), it serves as a major setting for Chapters 5-11 of Pokemon Amber. The Gym Leader position is currently vacant following the previous leader's decline; Erika is the leading candidate for succession.

## Overview

Celadon Gym functions as a multi-purpose institution offering badge challenges, entertainment battles, training facilities, and elite Ace Challenges. Governed by a board of noble families, it operates as both a battling institution and a social one — staffed primarily by daughters of Celadon's aristocratic clans, maintaining the refined Grass-type tradition.

**Type Specialty:** Grass  
**Gym Leader:** Vacant — previous leader stepped down (see [Gym Leader Succession](#gym-leader-succession) below). Erika is the leading candidate.  
**Badge:** Rainbow Badge  
**Area Certification:** Certifies trainers for Celadon City and surrounding forest/garden areas  
**Staff:** 30+ trainers plus administrative and security personnel

### Rainbow Badge Challenge

Celadon Gym issues badges certifying trainers for Celadon-area work (₽5,000+ per attempt).

**Badge Challenge Process:**
- Battle Gym Leader Erika or designated gym trainer(s)
- Fixed difficulty based on Celadon area's threats (Grass-types, forest Pokemon, urban gardens)
- Everyone faces similar challenge (tests area competency, not personalized)
- Win = earn Rainbow Badge for Celadon area
- Badge grants access to Celadon-area quests

**What Rainbow Badge Certifies:**
- Competency handling Celadon-area Pokemon (Grass-types, forest species)
- Forest navigation and garden ecosystem management
- Can safely work in Celadon City and surrounding areas
- Qualifies for quests in Celadon region

**For Pokemon League:**
- Counts as one of 8 required badges for Championship entry
- Multiple attempts allowed (each requires new fee)
- Traditional prestige as one of the original 8 Kanto gyms

### Gym League Challenges

Public entertainment battles:
- Regular tournaments (weekly or monthly)
- Broadcasted locally
- Spectators pay ₽10,000
- Participants compete for prize pools
- Earns League Points toward Championship qualification

## Governance

Celadon Gym is governed by a **board of noble families** — the same aristocratic clans that dominate Celadon's social and commercial life. The gym is as much a social institution as a battling one.

**Board structure:** Several noble families hold seats. They license Gym Leaders, audit staff, oversee operations, and move slowly through politics. Erika's family holds a seat.

**Noble-family culture:** The gym is staffed largely by daughters of these families, trained in the refined Grass-type tradition and traditional aesthetics. The kimono uniforms, the emphasis on grace and composure, the greenhouse gardens — all reflect this aristocratic character. Trainers who don't fit the mold (like Mary) are tolerated for their skill but not socially accepted.

**Board powers:** Can suspend trainers, gatekeep Gym Leader succession, and enforce institutional image standards. Mary's suspension for fighting too aggressively is an example — the stated reason is conduct, but the real issue is cultural fit.

## Gym Leader Succession

### The Previous Gym Leader

The previous Gym Leader — the old man now seen sitting on a bench outside the gym, peering through windows — was once a powerful and respected figure. He held the title for decades. He is not connected to any of the noble families; his authority came from his own strength and tenure.

**What happened:** He didn't fall in a scandal. He just got old. His mind went — slowly, visibly, embarrassingly. The filters came off. The dignified leader became the old man muttering about the girls in the gym. Not predatory — senile. The gym's prestige eroded under him as he became less capable and more erratic.

**Why removal took so long:** Stripping a sitting GL's title is politically and institutionally difficult. The League is decentralized (gym councils ARE the government — see [Evolution of Pokemon Gyms](../../../lore/history/gym-evolution.md)). There's no clean mechanism for forced removal when the problem is decline rather than misconduct. The board had to build consensus across multiple noble families, each with their own candidate preferences. By the time he formally stepped down, the succession had already become a political contest.

**The corruption underneath:** The old man presided over decades of the [Factory Ward](./factory-ward.md)'s neglect. Whether he actively enabled Giovanni's presence in Celadon or simply didn't care is unclear — but Team Rocket's Game Corner and deeper institutional reach in the city were established on his watch. The corruption isn't his personal scandal; it's the system the noble families built and maintained while he looked the other way.

**Current status:** Stripped of Pokemon and operational authority. Wanders the gym grounds out of habit. Sits on the bench near the entrance. The "creepy bench-dweller" Amber avoids in [Ch5][ch5].

**Canon basis:** Based on the unnamed old man NPC who stands outside Celadon Gym in every Kanto game, peering through the window. Original dialogue: "Heheh! This Gym is great! It's full of women!" ([Bulbapedia][bulbapedia-celadon-gym]). No canon predecessor to Erika exists — this is entirely original.

### Erika's Candidacy

Erika (~17) is the leading candidate to succeed as Gym Leader. Her family holds a board seat. She already runs the gym's daily operations — investigations, staff management, HP UP production, medical treatment.

**What blocks her:** The board moves slowly. Multiple families have preferences. Erika is young ("She's way too young to be Gym Leader" — Mary, [Ch10][ch10]). And Erika's willingness to bend rules — treating Factory Ward kids for free, in secret — suggests she may not be the compliant leader some board members want.

**Timeline:** Erika becomes Gym Leader around age 20, after a ~3 year timeskip. One of the youngest GLs in Kanto history.

## Commercial Operations

The gym is not just a battling institution — it's a business. The noble families on the board invested in an enterprise with two major product lines, both derived from the greenhouse Pokemon. This is what makes Celadon Gym wealthy and politically significant.

### Perfumes (Oddish / Gloom line)

Celadon Gym's public-facing product. In [canon][bulbapedia-ep026], Erika runs a perfumery making scents from Pokemon essences — specifically Gloom's. The gym teaches perfume manufacturing alongside trainer development.

In this story, the perfume operation uses Oddish and Gloom from the greenhouse. The aromatic compounds from their spores and secretions are refined into Celadon's signature perfume line. This is the gym's visible brand — the one visitors know about, the one tied to the refined noble-lady aesthetic.

**Why it matters:** Perfumes are the public explanation for why the greenhouse exists, why Oddish are cultivated, and why the gym smells like flowers. It's also cover for the more valuable product line.

### HP UP (Paras / Parasect tochukaso)

The gym's real money-maker. HP UP — the Pokemon health supplement sold at ₽9,800/bottle at the Celadon Department Store — is produced from **tochukaso**, the parasitic mushroom that grows on [Paras and Parasect][bulbapedia-tochukaso].

**Canon basis:** Tochukaso spores canonically make "herbal medicine which can be used to extend life" ([Bulbapedia][bulbapedia-tochukaso]). In [the anime][bulbapedia-ep044], Cassandra evolves her Paras specifically to harvest tochukaso spores for a "miracle potion" that improves attack power, defenses, concentration, determination, and intellect. That's HP UP.

**How it works in this story:**
- The gym used to produce HP UP from Oddish spores (older, less effective formula)
- They switched to Paras tochukaso mushrooms — better efficacy, stronger medicinal properties
- The formula change is a trade secret. Kayla almost blew it in Ch8: "Oddish isn't even—" before Erika shut her down ([Ch8][ch8])
- Parasect already lives in the greenhouse (seen in Ch8 security footage chasing Kaede)
- HP UP is a Pokemon supplement that enhances natural durability over time — it does NOT work on humans and could be dangerous ([Ch10][ch10])

**Kaede's mistake:** She stole the Oddish thinking its spores were the key HP UP ingredient, hoping to "extend [Hiro's] life." She was wrong on two counts — wrong Pokemon (it's Paras now, not Oddish) and wrong species (HP UP doesn't work on humans). But Oddish's Sweet Scent spores did genuinely ease Hiro's Sludge Fever breathing, which is why Erika let the Oddish stay.

**Why it's secret:** Proprietary formula = competitive advantage. If competitors knew it was tochukaso, they could source their own Paras and undercut Celadon's market. Erika's instinct to frame the Oddish theft as "corporate espionage" shows she's thinking about this constantly.

### The Greenhouse

The greenhouse serves both product lines:

- **Oddish / Gloom** — aromatic compounds for perfumes (public)
- **Paras / Parasect** — tochukaso mushrooms for HP UP (secret, restricted area)
- **Other Grass-types** — Vileplume, Combee, Bellsprout, etc. for training, ecosystem maintenance, and berry/herb cultivation
- **Herbal medicine supplies** — Erika's personal medical practice draws from greenhouse plants (she treats Hiro's Sludge Fever with greenhouse herbs in [Ch10][ch10])

The "RESTRICTED" sign on the greenhouse isn't just about Pokemon safety — it's protecting trade secrets.

# Factory Ward

The **Factory Ward** is Celadon City's industrial district — the underbelly that the garden city pretends doesn't exist. Polluted, impoverished, and neglected by the noble families who govern Celadon's institutions.

> **Design inspiration:** Zaun from *Arcane* — the toxic, forgotten underside of a prosperous city. The Factory Ward exists because Celadon's wealth requires somewhere to dump its costs.

## Overview

The Factory Ward is where Celadon puts what it doesn't want to look at: industrial waste, low-income housing, unregistered residents, and recurring public health crises. The noble families on the [Celadon Gym](./celadon-gym.md) board and the city's commercial elite maintain the garden city's beautiful surface by externalizing costs to this district.

The ward is not a separate city — it's part of Celadon. That's the point. The same government that funds ginkgo-lined avenues and greenhouse gardens lets the Factory Ward's residents breathe factory smoke and die of Sludge Fever.

## Conditions

**Housing:** Sparse apartments, cardboard-patched windows, thin mattresses, bare walls. "Single window clouded by factory smoke." ([Ch10][ch10])

**Health:** Sludge Fever is recurring — Erika says "It's been spreading through the Factory Ward *again*" ([Ch10][ch10]), meaning this isn't new. The illness is environmental, linked to industrial pollution. Residents lack insurance and can't access the formal medical system.

**Services:** No gym-level medical care, no insurance coverage, no registration for many residents. Kaede and Hiro have no valid registration, which means no hospital access. The formal systems that serve upper Celadon don't reach here.

## Political Structure

The Factory Ward's neglect is not an accident — it's structural.

**Noble family complicity:** The families who sit on the gym board and run Celadon's institutions benefit from cheap industrial output and low-cost labor. They don't actively oppress the Factory Ward — they just don't fund services, don't extend medical coverage, and don't ask questions about pollution levels. Neglect, not malice.

**Giovanni / Team Rocket:** Rocket fills the vacuum the noble families created. The Game Corner operates as a front in upper Celadon, but Rocket's real institutional reach extends into the Factory Ward — where there's no oversight, no police presence, and desperate people willing to work for anyone who pays. The ward is Rocket's recruiting ground and operational base.

**The old Gym Leader:** The previous GL — now senile and wandering the gym grounds — presided over decades of this arrangement. Whether he actively enabled it or simply didn't care is an open question. The rot isn't one man's scandal; it's the system the noble families built and maintained.

## Known Residents

**Kaede** — Stole an Oddish from the gym greenhouse, believing its spores could help her sick brother. Lives in the Factory Ward in poverty. No insurance, no registration. See [Kaede](../../characters/kaede.md).

**Hiro** — Kaede's six-year-old brother. Sick with Sludge Fever. Treated by Erika for free, in secret, outside gym procedures. ([Ch10][ch10])

## Role in Story

### Pre-Journey (Ch10)

Amber, Mary, and Erika travel to the Factory Ward to verify Kaede's story and treat Hiro. Amber sees the contrast between the gym's polished facilities and the ward's poverty firsthand. Erika treats Hiro for free and tells Kaede: "Just... don't mention this to anyone. The gym has procedures." ([Ch10][ch10])

This is the first time Amber sees what's underneath Celadon's surface. She doesn't act on it — she's 10 and has no power. But she sees it.

### Arc 2 / Arc 6 (Journey)

The Factory Ward's conditions are part of the Celadon arc's political tension. When Amber returns to Celadon at 13 and again at 14, the ward is still there, still rotting. Whether Erika — now pursuing the GL title — will actually change anything, or whether she'll inherit the system as-is, is an open question.

Team Rocket's presence in the Factory Ward connects to the Vee thread (Rocket experiment cycling between Eeveelutions, first encountered in Arc 2).

## Thematic Significance

The Factory Ward is what "garden city" really means — a city that cultivates beauty by burying its ugliness. The noble families aren't villains in a dramatic sense. They're institutions that benefit from not looking. Giovanni isn't corrupting Celadon — he's exploiting a gap that was already there.

Erika's decision to treat Hiro for free, in secret, shows she sees the problem. Whether she'll fix it when she has power — or whether the system will absorb her too — is the Celadon arc's central question.

## Open Questions

- How deep is Rocket's institutional reach in the Factory Ward?
- Is Sludge Fever directly caused by Rocket operations, or is it just industrial pollution the city ignores?
- Does Erika's path to GL require her to confront the Factory Ward problem, or can she take the title while leaving the system intact?
- Does the Factory Ward have its own informal power structures (community leaders, underground economy)?

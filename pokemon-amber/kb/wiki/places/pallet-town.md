# Pallet Town

**Pallet Town** is a small coastal town in southwestern Kanto, known as the location of Professor Oak's prestigious Pokemon research laboratory. It serves as a key setting for Chapters 11-18 of Pokemon Amber.

## Canonical Interpretations

### Games & Anime

In Pokemon canon, Pallet Town is extremely small, with a population of only 8 residents (excluding the player and rival) ([Bulbapedia](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pallet_Town)). The town is described as "fairly new and quiet" and notably lacks standard amenities like Pokemon Centers or Gyms.

**Canon features:**
- Location: Southwestern Kanto
- Professor Oak's Laboratory (southeast corner)
- Player's house and rival's house
- Route 1 connection north (to Viridian City)
- Route 21 connection south (water route to Cinnabar Island)
- Town slogan: "Shades of your journey await"
- Starting point for new trainers receiving starter Pokemon

Fishing is the primary resource-gathering activity mentioned, with water routes adjacent to town where trainers can catch Pokemon like Magikarp, Tentacool, and Poliwag.

## Pokemon Ambertwo's Interpretation

This story significantly expands Pallet Town from its canonical tiny size to a functional small town, necessary to support the narrative elements (Delia's restaurant, community, etc.).

**Story-specific changes:**

**Region:** Southwest Kanto (coastal) - same as canon
**Population:** A few thousand residents - **EXPANDED from canon's 8 residents**
**Primary Industries:** Fishing (canon basis), Pokemon research (Oak's Lab)
**Notable Features:** Oak's Pokemon Research Laboratory (canon), fishing port (**story addition**), Pallet House restaurant (**story addition**), small-town community (**story addition**)

The story portrays Pallet Town as a viable fishing community with an active harbor, while maintaining its identity as Oak's hometown and the starting point for trainers' journeys.

**Narrative Rationale:**
Canon Pallet Town's 8 residents cannot support the story's requirements (Delia running a restaurant, community dynamics, witnesses to events, funeral attendees, etc.). The expansion maintains Pallet Town's "small town" character while creating a functional setting for Chapters 11-18.

## Geography & Layout

**Note:** The following geography is story-specific, expanding significantly beyond canon's minimal town structure.

### Town Structure

Pallet Town is built along the coast with a traditional small-town layout:

**Harbor/Fishing District:**
- Active fishing port with daily catches
- Fishing boats and equipment
- Fish market (morning activity)
- Traditional fishing community

**Central Town:**
- Main residential area
- Local businesses including Pallet House (breakfast restaurant/diner)
- Mix of older homes and some newer construction near Oak's lab
- Small-town atmosphere with neighbors knowing each other

**Oak's Lab District:**
- Professor Oak's research facility (major landmark)
- Wealthier/important residents cluster in this area
- Oak's personal residence
- Attracted researchers, trainers, and visiting academics

### Notable Locations

**Professor Oak's Pokemon Research Laboratory:**
The town's most prestigious institution, established by former Champion Samuel Oak (a Pallet Town native). The lab serves as:
- Pokemon research facility
- Starter Pokemon distribution center for beginning trainers
- Major economic driver for the town
- Destination for visiting researchers and trainers

**Pallet House:**
Breakfast restaurant/diner owned and operated by Delia Ketchum (inherited from her mother). Serves:
- Local fishermen (early morning crowd)
- Lab workers and researchers
- Townspeople
- Visitors to Oak's lab

Small family operation providing decent business from the mixed customer base ([Ch.11-18](../../../story/chapter11/11chapter.md)).

**Ketchum-Oak Residence:**
Modest blue house with garden, home to Delia, Stephen, and Ash Ketchum. Features:
- Carefully tended flower garden
- Backyard training area (for Ash and Poliwag)
- Stephen's eight gym badges displayed above fireplace
- Warm, lived-in family home

Destroyed by fire in Chapter 14-15 during Dr. Fuji's attack ([Ch.14-15](../../../story/chapter14/14chapter.md)).

## Economy

Pallet Town maintains a stable small-town economy with two main pillars:

**Fishing Industry:**
- Active and thriving coastal fishing
- Provides employment for significant portion of population
- Daily fish market
- Traditional livelihood predating Oak's lab

**Oak's Lab Influence:**
- Major economic boost to the town
- Attracts researchers, trainers, visitors
- Creates demand for local services (restaurants, lodging, supplies)
- Brought wealthier residents to the area
- Not the only thing keeping town alive, but significant contributor

The combination creates a functional small town with mix of working-class fishermen and research-connected residents.

## Community Character

**Small-Town Dynamics:**
- Close-knit community where residents know each other
- Mix of long-time fishing families and newer lab-connected residents
- Peaceful, quiet atmosphere
- Traditional values with modern research presence

**Oak's Influence:**
As a Pallet Town native who became Champion and established his lab here, Oak holds significant respect and influence in the community. His presence elevated the town's profile while maintaining its small-town character.

## Role in Story

Pallet Town becomes a central location in Chapters 11-18:

**Chapter 11:**
- Dr. Fuji brings Amber to Delia's home, forcing confrontation about their shared past
- Fuji abandons Amber in Delia's garden
- Introduction of Stephen Ketchum and young Ash ([Ch.11](../../../story/chapter11/11chapter.md))

**Chapter 12:**
- Amber's brief time living with the Ketchum-Oak family
- Domestic life in Pallet Town
- Stephen's past as trainer hinted at ([Ch.12](../../../story/chapter12/12chapter.md))

**Chapter 13:**
- Stephen murdered by Dr. Fuji on forest path outside town
- Fuji's delusional plan to "recreate" his family
- Town's peaceful atmosphere shattered ([Ch.13](../../../story/chapter13/13chapter.md))

**Chapter 14-15:**
- Dr. Fuji's attack on Ketchum home
- House burned down
- Battle between Oak and Fuji in residential area
- Community witnesses the violence
- Officer Jenny and local police respond ([Ch.14-15](../../../story/chapter14/14chapter.md))

**Chapter 17-18:**
- Aftermath of violence
- Delia, Ash, and Amber staying at Oak's house
- Pallet House restaurant closed then reopened
- Community awareness of tragedy
- Stephen's funeral
- Amber's eventual departure

The town serves as both idyllic small-town setting and site of tragic violence, showing how Dr. Fuji's obsession destroys peaceful lives.

## Connections

**Route 1:**
Connects Pallet Town to Viridian City to the north. Traditional route for beginning trainers starting their journey.

**Viridian City:**
Nearest major city, accessible via Route 1. Location of Viridian Forest and Viridian Gym.

**Regional Significance:**
While small, Pallet Town holds regional importance due to Oak's lab and his role as former Champion. Beginning trainers often start their journey here after receiving starter Pokemon from Oak.

## Trivia & Notes

**Canon details:**
- Pallet Town's canonical population is 8 residents (games/anime)
- Located in southwestern Kanto with Routes 1 (north) and 21 (south)
- Professor Oak's lab is in the southeast corner of town
- Fishing is mentioned as resource-gathering activity in canon
- Town lacks Pokemon Center and Gym in canon

**Story-specific details:**
- Population expanded to "a few thousand" to support narrative (restaurants, community, witnesses)
- Pallet House restaurant based on Takeshi Shudō's novelization concept (Delia runs restaurant in novelization, though not game/anime canon)
- Fishing industry expanded from canon's basic fishing into active port economy
- Oak being Pallet Town native who returned to establish lab (implied in canon, made explicit here)
- Community dynamics and multiple residents necessary for Chapters 11-18 events
- Town experienced tragedy when Dr. Fuji's violence destroyed the Ketchum home and murdered Stephen

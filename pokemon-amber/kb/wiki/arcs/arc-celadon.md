# Future Arc: Celadon

**Status:** Seed with political structure established. Plot details TBD.

> **Design inspiration:** Piltover/Zaun from *Arcane* — a beautiful city whose wealth is maintained by neglecting its industrial underbelly.

## Political Structure (Settled)

### The Two Celadons

Celadon is divided between the **upper city** (gardens, gym, department store, noble families) and the **[Factory Ward](../places/celadon-city/factory-ward.md)** (industrial pollution, poverty, Sludge Fever, no services). The noble families who govern the gym and dominate the city's institutions maintain this divide through neglect, not active oppression. They just don't fund services, don't extend medical coverage, and don't ask about pollution levels.

Giovanni / Team Rocket fills the vacuum. The Game Corner is the visible front; deeper institutional reach extends into the Factory Ward where there's no oversight and desperate people willing to work for anyone who pays.

### Gym Leader Succession

The previous GL — the old man now sitting on a bench peering through the gym's windows — was once powerful and respected. He went senile with age. Lost his filters. Became an embarrassment. His decline exposed (or at least failed to conceal) the structural rot he'd presided over for decades. He's now stripped of Pokemon and operational authority but wasn't removed through any clean mechanism — the process was slow, political, and messy.

The GL position is **currently vacant**. The board can't agree on a successor because each noble family has preferences and the political stakes are high.

**Erika (~17)** is the leading candidate. Her family holds a board seat. She already runs daily operations. But she's young, and her willingness to bend rules (treating Factory Ward kids for free in secret) suggests she may not be the compliant leader some board members want. She becomes GL around age 20 — one of the youngest in Kanto history.

### The Noble Families

The gym board is composed of several noble families. The gym is as much a social institution as a battling one — staffed by their daughters, maintaining traditional aesthetics. The corruption isn't a scandal — it's structural. These families benefit from the Factory Ward's cheap industrial output while cultivating the garden city's beautiful surface.

**Open:** Is the family system itself divided on Rocket? Do some families have direct dealings with Giovanni, or do they all just benefit from looking the other way?

## Core Threads

### Erika's Gym Leader Path

Erika is working toward officially becoming Celadon's gym leader. The obstacles are now clearer:

- **Board politics:** Multiple noble families with competing candidates and preferences. Slow consensus-building.
- **Age:** "She's way too young to be Gym Leader" ([Ch10][ch10]). The board uses this as cover for political delay.
- **Her character:** Erika bends rules when people need help. She treats Factory Ward kids for free, in secret, outside gym procedures. This is who she is — but it's also what makes some board members nervous. Will she rock the boat?
- **The central question:** Does Erika inherit the system as-is (beautiful gym, rotting ward), or does she actually try to change something? Can she take the title while confronting what it was built on?

### Team Rocket in Celadon

Rocket has significant presence — Game Corner as a front operation, deeper institutional reach into the Factory Ward. Their interest in Erika's position (or the vacuum around it) is part of the arc's tension.

The Factory Ward is Rocket's recruiting ground and operational base. Sludge Fever may or may not be directly linked to Rocket operations — or it could just be industrial pollution the city ignores. Either way, Rocket thrives in the gap the noble families created.

### Vee's Role

Vee is somehow connected to the Erika plotline — possibly:
- Vee has information about Rocket's Celadon operation from its time as a test subject
- Vee as evidence of what Rocket is doing in the city, which Amber uses to support Erika
- Vee's presence during the Rocket escalation (first encounter) is what pulls Amber into the larger Celadon politics

## Two-Visit Structure

**Visit 1 (Arc 2, age 13, pre-4Ever):**
- Amber passes through Celadon pursuing badges, encounters Vee
- Gets drawn into the Erika/Rocket situation peripherally
- Helps Vee fight off a Rocket escalation, leaves without recruiting Vee
- Seeds the Erika arc and Factory Ward thread without resolving them
- Amber already knows the Factory Ward from Ch10 (age 10) — Kaede, Hiro, Sludge Fever

**Visit 2 (Arc 6, age 14, post-4Ever):**
- Amber returns deliberately to find Vee
- The Erika arc may be ongoing or reach its climax here
- Amber recruits Vee; the shared Rocket enemy is the foundation
- Erika's GL succession may resolve here or remain in progress
- **Kaede is now TR.** Erika remembers the girl whose brother she saved. Kaede sees the Gym Leader who let the Factory Ward rot. Their collision is a key Celadon beat --- structural resentment, not personal betrayal (they were strangers who met through the heist, not childhood friends)

## Open Questions

- Does Erika confront the Factory Ward problem as part of her GL path, or does that come later?
- What is the noble families' relationship to Rocket — active complicity, passive benefit, or genuine ignorance?
- Does Amber's help have a cost — does she get pulled deeper into Celadon politics than she wants?
- Does Erika know about Vee's existence / Rocket's experiments in the city?
- Is Sludge Fever caused by Rocket operations, or just industrial neglect?
- Does the Factory Ward have its own informal power structures?
- What role does Mary play in the Celadon arc visits? (She's gone by Visit 2 — wandering adventurer by age 18.)

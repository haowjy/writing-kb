# Late-Saga Dimensional Displacement Bridge

Current planning direction for the brief mundane-world displacement that hands Amber into Alola. The emotional shape is fairly strong; the exact cosmology and trigger remain partly open.

## Locked Direction

- **Placement:** This happens after **Kalos** and before **Alola**, **Delta Episode**, and **the kingdom raid (Visit 2)**.
- **Trigger family:** The current preferred family is **Kalos-side Mega Dimension fallout** or another closely related late-saga dimensional rupture. Exact mechanics remain open.
- **Destination:** Amber is displaced into the **actual mundane real world**, not an alternate Earth where Pokemon exist as reduced pets or hidden fauna.
- **Identity:** She remains physically **Amber**, not "reverted" into her pre-isekai self.
- **World condition:** No Pokemon exist there except as media, games, and franchise material.
- **Duration:** Brief. Exact length remains flexible, but this is not a new long-form premise.
- **Narrative function:** This is an emotional wound, identity test, and recontextualization of home. It is not the story changing genres permanently.
- **Extraction:** Aether / Lusamine pull Amber back out through Ultra Wormhole technology, which becomes the bridge into Alola / Ultra Space material.
- **Home question:** This bridge probably already answers the "return to the old world" emotional question. A second major old-world choice later is likely unnecessary.

## What the Real-World Interlude Should Feel Like

The point is not bureaucratic realism. The point is dislocation.

- Avoid a long orphanage, jail, court, or paperwork subplot.
- Prefer pragmatic survival beats: possible hospital escape, limited gear, no valid identity, figuring out how to eat, shelter, and move for a short period.
- If she has equipment, it should be partly unusable or only marginally useful in mundane Earth conditions.
- Pokemon media should feel **hollow and painful** to her. Games, anime, and cards are only a child's map of the living world she now knows, not a substitute for it.

## Current Preferred Handoff Into Alola

The current preferred version is:

- Amber is recovered during **Aether Ultra Wormhole experiments**.
- Her survival as a humanoid cross-world anomaly gives **Lusamine** hope that **Mohn** could still be alive somewhere.
- Lusamine focuses on Amber because Amber proves cross-world survival is possible and carries memories of another world.
- Amber's recovery either destabilizes the experiment or coincides with the threshold failure that follows.
- Another wormhole event then leads into the **Lusamine / Nihilego** crisis.
- In the aftermath, Aether blames or fears Amber as an anomaly, contaminant, saboteur, or Ultra Beast-adjacent risk.

The technical chain is still open. The durable part is that the extraction does **not** restore Amber to safety. It hands her directly into an Alola arc built on suspicion, exhaustion, and flight.

## Real-World Echo Beat

One preferred symbolic beat is Amber reaching her old dorm, belongings, or an equivalent piece of her former life and encountering an old Pokemon game, emulator, or DS cartridge. The save or battle state may echo her Chapter 1 Gyarados fear in some humiliating, game-logic way.

This beat should remain **narrative rhyme, not magical causation**. Aether detects or locks onto Amber because of Amber herself — body, residue, dimensional signature, or related trace — not because of the game.

## Why This Fits the Existing Lore Direction

Conversation research pointed toward a useful support structure from **Legends Z-A / Mega Dimension / Hyperspace Lumiose** material:

- spatial distortion and dimensional instability around Mega phenomena
- Hoopa / hyperspace language in Kalos-side lore
- Darkrai tied to subconscious thought, memory, nightmare logic, and unstable interior worlds

This supports the idea that **Mega-Evolution-adjacent dimensional instability** can produce a real cross-world rupture without requiring the story to claim that every mechanic is already fully solved science in-world.

This is support, not a total canon lock on the exact mechanism.

## Open Mechanics

- What exact moment causes the rupture: Kalos Mega-Dimension failure, Hyperspace Lumiose-adjacent instability, cumulative cross-dimensional stress, or something else
- Does Amber fall straight into the mundane world, or through a brief Ultra-space-like transit first
- How much Darkrai-, dream-, or later kingdom-adjacent residue should still matter in the broader dimensional ecology, even if it is no longer the direct trigger
- How does Aether detect or target her specifically
- How long does Amber spend in the mundane world before Aether recovers her
- Does Amber's arrival directly destabilize the experiment, or does it happen during an already unstable threshold
- Is Lusamine best framed as pulled into, corrupted by, or partially merged with Nihilego

## Relationship to Other Arcs

- **Kalos** should now be the main lead-in because it lets Mega mastery and dimensional lore become groundwork rather than retroactive explanation.
- **Alola / Ultra Space** begins as the recovery and consequences phase, with Lusamine useful but dangerous rather than simply benevolent.
- **Delta Episode** follows because Amber's real-world displacement and Alola experience give her the ethical grounds to oppose dimensional-disposal plans and support Zinnia's claim on the Rayquaza crisis.
- **The kingdom raid (Visit 2)** comes later, strengthened by Alola and Delta rather than causing them.
- **Endgame** stays later. This bridge is not the climax; it is part of how the world gets bigger and more dangerous before the climax.

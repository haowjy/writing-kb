# Amber: Spoken Dialogue and Interaction

Load this file when Amber speaks aloud in any scene. For her narration/internal voice, load `char-amber-narration.md`.

---

## The Core Dynamic

Amber was a college-age Pokemon fan who never really grew out of it. The gap between her previous self and her child presentation is narrower than it might seem — her personality was already pretty compatible with childhood. Her spoken dialogue shifts register based on context, audience, and emotional state — she's genuinely navigating different social situations from a child's power position with adult analytical capability.

The narration carries most of the comic voice. Her spoken dialogue is simpler, with more hedging and filler. The narration does the identity work *around* her spoken lines — the interplay between what she says and what she thinks is where the voice lives.

---

## Register by Audience

**Authority figures** — hedging, filler, deference. She *is* in a child's power position:
> "Um... I might be able to help with identifying her. Since you've already seen what my Ditto can do... Would it be okay if I used it?" (Ch. 8)

**Peers (Ash, Mary)** — chattier, more impulsive, more relaxed:
> "I'm fine! Totally fine. Super fine. Never been better. Did you know your hair looks like spaghetti when it moves?" (Ch. 9)

**Fuji** — either shrinks under the parental dynamic or deliberately weaponizes the adult register:
> "What happened to... Dr. Fuji?" (Ch. 17) — shrunk
> "All this time, you've been dressing a complete stranger in your dead daughter's clothes. Think about how fucked up that is." (Ch. 15) — deliberate adult register

**Teaching mode (Ash)** — patient older sibling, adult knowledge in accessible wrapper:
> "Okay, let's try this step by step. First rule---always start with the ones..." (Ch. 14)

---

## The Ch. 15 Confrontation

The least child-coded speech Amber gives aloud. Syntax lengthens, logic sharpens, insults become surgical:
> "I'm not your daughter. I never was." (Ch. 15)
> "You're a failure. A failure of a scientist, a husband, a father." (Ch. 15)

Amber deliberately speaks from the adult register here and weaponizes it. That deliberateness is what makes the scene land.

---

## Dialogue Mechanics

Amber's lines are frequently interleaved with narration that carries the real voice. Action beats: waves hands, freezes, flinches, blurts, bounces, clutches people tighter.

# Mechanical Tics — Repeated Phrases

**Scope**: Word/phrase-level repetition across entire manuscript
**Severity**: Low-moderate individually, cumulative effect on prose freshness

---

## High-Frequency Repeated Phrases

| Phrase | Count | Issue |
|--------|-------|-------|
| "for a moment" | 29 | Filler beat. Rarely adds meaning. |
| "cut through the" | 19 | Stock action phrase. Voices "cut through" air, night, silence. |
| "before I could" | 16 | Reactive delay construction. Amber is constantly interrupted. |
| "through the air" | 15 | Generic spatial marker for projectiles, sound, smell. |
| "for a long moment" | 8 | Intensified version of "for a moment." |

### Examples

> "For a moment, I thought..." (Ch. 1)
> "...before I could stop..." (Ch. 14, Ch. 15)
> "Dr. Fuji's voice knifed through the night." (Ch. 15) — same construction family as "cut through the"

---

## High-Frequency Body Language / Perception Anchors

| Bigram | Count | Issue |
|--------|-------|-------|
| "his voice" | 42 | Default attribution anchor. |
| "the air" | 41 | Catch-all spatial reference. |
| "his eyes" | 36 | Default external character detail. |
| "her eyes" | 28 | Same pattern, different character. |

These function as "camera anchors" — the prose reaches for the same body parts and spatial references to ground scenes. Not all instances are problematic, but the density suggests over-reliance on eyes-and-voice as default character descriptors.

---

## Hedge Markers

| Word | Count | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| "probably" | 31 | Intentional in Amber's voice (uncertainty), but high count |
| "kind of" | 27 | Same |
| "maybe" | 23 | Same |

These are partially intentional — Amber's adult-processing-through-child-uncertainty voice uses hedging. But 81 hedge markers across 49k words may dilute the prose in scenes where Amber should sound more decisive.

---

## Recommended Action

- Search-and-evaluate "for a moment" — most can be cut or replaced with specific duration/action
- Vary the "cut through" family — find different verbs for sound/voice/sensation traversing space
- Review "before I could" — some instances encode the child-body-can't-keep-up theme (keep those); others are just delayed-reaction filler (cut those)
- Audit "his/her eyes" density in conversation scenes — find other physical details to anchor attribution

# Emotional Scene Inconsistency

**Scope**: Scene-level technique across middle chapters
**Severity**: Moderate — the strongest emotional scenes are very strong, but the technique isn't always committed to

---

## The Problem

The prose has a clear, distinctive emotional technique (physical sensation over naming, internal monologue dropout, silence and omission). It's deployed effectively in:
- Ch. 2 bathroom scene
- Ch. 10 train ending
- Ch. 15 Electabuzz death and confrontation

But middle chapters don't fully commit to this register when they should.

---

## Ch. 11: The Primary Gap

Ch. 11 (Pallet Town reveal, Delia rejecting the clone, Fuji's breakdown) has high-stakes emotional content, but Amber is largely passive. The prose doesn't give us her interiority during the confrontation — we get observation, not feeling.

**What's missing**: Amber watching Delia reject her should be one of the most emotionally devastating moments in the story. The prose treats it as reportage. Where's the body response? The stopped fragments? The internal monologue dropout that signals overwhelm?

**Possible intent**: Amber may be in shock, which would explain the observational distance. But if so, the shock itself needs to be rendered physically — not just implied by the absence of feeling.

---

## Ch. 17: Secondary Gap

Ch. 17 is more observational and distant than the emotional content warrants. Amber is processing aftermath, but the prose stays in commentary mode rather than shifting into the emotional register.

---

## What "Committing to the Register" Means

When the technique works (Ch. 2, Ch. 15), the prose:
1. Drops the game-knowledge meta-commentary entirely
2. Renders emotion through physical sensation, not labels
3. Uses stopped fragments, not kinetic ones
4. Carries weight through silence and omission
5. Lets objects stand in for feelings

When the technique doesn't commit, the prose stays in Amber's default analytical/observational mode during moments that deserve the full emotional apparatus.

---

## Recommended Action

- Review Ch. 11's Pallet Town confrontation for emotional gaps — add body response, physical sensation, stopped fragments where Amber is witnessing her own rejection
- Review Ch. 17's aftermath scenes for the same — the chapter may need moments where the commentary voice drops out and the body reports
- Check whether the intent was deliberate distance (shock, numbness) and if so, render that state physically rather than leaving it as an absence of technique

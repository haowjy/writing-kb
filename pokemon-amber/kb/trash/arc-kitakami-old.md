# Future Arc: Kitakami I (Arc 10)

**Amber age:** 16 | **Status:** Kitakami I key beats largely locked. Kitakami II return skeleton established, but many execution details remain open.

## What It Is

Kitakami is a small, completely isolated village (population ~55) in a mountainous region surrounded by dense Dragon-type Pokemon territory. Nobody in the outside world knows it exists — only rumors. The dragon gauntlet (Gible/Gabite/Garchomp dens, Jangmo-o/Hakamo-o/Kommo-o packs) has kept it inaccessible for generations.

**Amber is the first outsider to reach it and discover it for the world.**

This is primarily a **training and discovery arc** — no major villain, no tournament. Amber is getting stronger. The difficulty is the world itself.

For Amber, Kitakami is not a generic dungeon-crawler destination. It is a sealed community, a dangerous promise, and eventually the place where Gardevoir pays a cost she has to come back for.

---

## Entry: The Dragon Gauntlet

Routes in are Dragon country:
- Gible/Gabite/Garchomp in the mountain passes (Crystal Pool, Oni Mountain)
- Jangmo-o/Hakamo-o/Kommo-o in the wilds and Paradise Barrens / Wistful Fields
- Fully evolved dragons deeper in

Most travelers turn back without knowing there's anything on the other side. Oak made it as far as Wistful Fields at age 17 before being driven back by a Kommo-o. He never found out what was there.

**How Amber gets through:** Partially strength — she's a Hoenn conference winner. But something else is at play. The dragons let her pass in a way that defies pure force. Connection to her Mew DNA is likely (see: Mew as protector, below).

**Gible acquisition:** Gible dens are in the mountain passes. If Amber earns a Gible here, it's one of the most narratively earned acquisitions of the saga. This Gible travels with her into Sinnoh.

## The Village

- Two settlements: Mossui Town (pop. ~17) and Kitakami Hall (pop. ~38)
- Deeply traditional; annual Festival of Masks
- Developed xenophobia over generations of isolation
- **Nobody leaves.** No human is supposed to enter.

### The Ancient History — Two Layers

Kitakami's mythology has two distinct layers the village doesn't fully understand themselves:

### Layer 1: The Mew Sacrifice (~3,000 years ago — Kalos War era)

During or after the Kalos War (3,000 years ago), legendary Pokemon were displaced and driven chaotic by the war's devastation. **Mega Darkrai** — drawn to the concentrated grief and death of that era — found Oni Mountain and began corrupting the territory. A **Mew died protecting the region**, creating the original seal that trapped Mega Darkrai within the mountain. Its sacrifice created a passive veil over Kitakami — not a living guardian, just the afterglow of something that gave everything.

The original Mew sacrifice/seal is the locked foundation. The later maintenance model is still open:

- **Open option:** Cresselia may have become part of the seal's long-term maintenance or dream/nightmare suppression later, without replacing Mew as the one who made the original sacrifice.
- **Open option:** Darkrai itself may subconsciously ward people away from Kitakami because it knows it is dangerous.

The seal held for 2,700 years. It didn't fully succeed — residual corruption lingered in the land, slowly seeping into susceptible Pokemon and people over centuries.

### Layer 2: Ogerpon and the Masked Man (~300 years ago)

As the Mew's seal weakened, the barrier around Kitakami became permeable. **This is how the masked man and Ogerpon found their way in** — two outsiders arriving into a place already quietly poisoned by ancient corruption.

The Loyal Three's cruelty wasn't purely Pecharunt's doing — they were already corrupted by residual Darkrai energy, making them susceptible to Pecharunt's Binding Mochi manipulation. The old couple's greed, Pecharunt's awakening, the Loyal Three's betrayal — all downstream of a seal that had been cracking for centuries.

Ogerpon's grief after the masked man's death, and her centuries of isolation on Oni Mountain, happened in a place where Mega Darkrai's corruption still lingers beneath the rock.

### Now (Amber's arrival)

The seal is weak enough that Amber can enter. The dragons in the gauntlet let her pass — partly strength (Hoenn conference winner), partly something else. Her trace Mew DNA resonates with what the Mew left behind. She didn't force her way in. Something in the mountain recognized her.

### What Amber Discovers

Amber uncovers the full truth:
- The Loyal Three mythology is a lie
- The Mew sacrifice and Mega Darkrai are real and ancient
- The seal is failing — Mega Darkrai is still there, still sealed, but weakening

She gets a glimpse of Mega Darkrai's presence. She realizes immediately she is **nowhere near strong enough** to face it. This is a future arc problem, not a now problem.

**What she does:** She reinforces the seal instinctively — something in her Mew DNA responds to what the Mew left behind. It's not deliberate, she doesn't fully understand it. It buys more time. Not a fix. A patch.

**Gardevoir stays:** The seal needs sustained psychic resonance to hold. Gardevoir chooses to remain. Amber can't argue against it — they both understand what's at stake. A quiet goodbye. Amber walks back through the dragon gauntlet alone.

**She tells no one.** Not Oak. Not Kyle. Nobody. She carries Kitakami, Mega Darkrai, and Gardevoir alone.

She tells the villagers she will come back and free them. She doesn't know when.

### The Village Opening

Amber convinces the villagers to officially open Kitakami to the outside world — or at least to allow people to leave freely. Their xenophobia is real but not malicious; they've been closed so long they forgot there was a choice. This is the diplomatic arc of Kitakami — not a battle, a conversation.

## Future Return: Kitakami II

Kitakami II is now the preferred shape of Amber's **first deliberate legendary-class raid.**

- It happens **after** Amber gains real Mega mastery and stronger allies, and now specifically **after Alola and the Delta Episode return to Hoenn**.
- The goal is not exploration for its own sake. The goal is to resolve the Mega Darkrai crisis, free Gardevoir and the village from the seal burden, and fulfill the promise Kitakami I created.
- The human team should stay small — roughly **4-6 people** Amber trusts who are individually very strong. This previews endgame coalition logic without becoming the whole endgame alliance.
- **Alonso likely belongs on the team.** Impossible places, expedition logic, rescue quests, and Mystery Dungeon-scale danger are his natural lane.
- Kitakami II should feel like a chosen return to an unpaid debt, not a random late-saga revisit.
- Alola should strengthen this arc. Amber returns with **Ultra Wormhole experience, other-world ethics, Aether/Lusamine lessons, and the Delta precedent of supporting the right specialist rather than leading every mythic crisis herself**.
- Kitakami II should now read as the **last major personal promise** Amber resolves before the full global war reveals itself.

## The Loyal Three

Amber encounters Okidogi, Munkidori, and Fezandipiti in the village. The village mythology treats them as heroes. **Amber knows the truth** — they're the villains of the story, the ones who stole Ogerpon's masks. She doesn't reveal this yet. The buried truth is seeded but not surfaced here.

---

## The Ogerpon Mythology (The Buried Truth)

The official village history: three heroic Pokemon (the Loyal Three) protected the village from an evil ogre. Worshipped for generations.

**The actual history:** Ogerpon and a man arrived as outsiders, were rejected for being different. A compassionate mask-maker gave them four masks. Pecharunt sent the Loyal Three to steal the masks. The man was injured protecting Ogerpon. Ogerpon drove the thieves away. Villagers misread the aftermath and built a mythology around the wrong side.

The truth was passed down only through the mask-maker's descendants. The rest of the village has been wrong for 300 years.

**Why this resonates with Amber:** She knows the games — she knows the Ogerpon truth before the village does. Themes of misremembered history and the outsider misjudged map directly onto her own story. She's an outsider (isekai'd, clone) who has been misread her whole existence.

### Ursaring → Ursaluna

Ursaring evolves into Ursaluna during the Kitakami arc. Canon requirement: Peat Block during a full moon. Peat Blocks are Hisui/ancient Sinnoh artifacts — a lore reason for one existing in Kitakami needs to be established (isolated village, ancient trade route, preserved relic). ### Pseudolegendaries (1-2)

Kitakami is non-government territory — acquisitions here are mostly free from visa restrictions (can be stabled without carry limit concerns). Amber's roster expands seriously.

- **Gible** — Dragon gauntlet acquisition. Most narratively earned pickup of the saga. Travels with Amber to Sinnoh.
- **Second pseudolegendary TBD** — Kommo-o line possible (confronted in gauntlet), Goodra possible (wetlands/rain adjacent), others TBD.

### Pikachu Handoff

On the road home from Kitakami, Amber finds a Pikachu that won't go in a ball. Recognizes it immediately. Travels with it briefly. Returns to Pallet Town. Tells Oak: *"Give it to Ash if he wakes up late."*

Ash is 14 — just starting his journey. The timing is exact.

## The Oak Payoff

Oak pushed into the Kitakami outskirts at age 17, was driven back by a Kommo-o, and never found out what was there. He has been telling this story for forty years. It has no ending.

After Amber discovers Kitakami, she tells Oak what was on the other side. He spent forty years wondering.

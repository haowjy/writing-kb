# Task: Analyze Character Dialogue Voices

Read all 17 chapters. Your job is to catalog how each character sounds in dialogue — their speech patterns, vocabulary, verbal tics, and how they differ from each other.

## Characters to focus on

- **Amber** — how she speaks aloud vs how she narrates. Code-switching between child-appropriate and adult-slipping.
- **Dr. Fuji** — scientific vocabulary, emotional guardedness, how he talks to Amber vs others
- **Professor Oak** — authority, warmth, political savvy
- **Erika** — formality level, Celadon gym leader bearing
- **Kayla** — age-appropriate speech, energy level, slang
- **Any other recurring characters** with enough dialogue to analyze

## What to analyze per character

- Vocabulary range and register
- Sentence length and complexity in dialogue
- Verbal tics or repeated phrases
- How they express emotion (direct, deflected, suppressed)
- How they talk differently to different people
- Action beats — what do they DO while talking?

## Cross-character patterns

- How does the author differentiate characters in dialogue-heavy scenes?
- Are there scenes where two characters' voices are hard to tell apart? (Problems to fix)
- How much does dialogue carry vs narration in conversation scenes?

Write your analysis to: .meridian/work/style-extraction/brainstorm/character-dialogue-voices.md

Quote extensively. For each character, provide at least 5-6 representative dialogue examples with chapter citations.

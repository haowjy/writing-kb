# Task: Analyze Mechanical Prose Patterns

Read all 17 chapters. Your job is to identify the measurable, mechanical patterns in the prose — the stuff you can count and categorize, not subjective quality judgments.

## What to measure/catalog

### Sentence patterns
- How do sentences typically open? (Subject-verb, participial phrase, prepositional, temporal, etc.)
- How varied are sentence openers within a paragraph? Within a chapter?
- Typical sentence length range — are there signature rhythms?

### Paragraph structure
- Typical paragraph length (sentences per paragraph)
- How often are single-sentence paragraphs used? For what effect?
- How do paragraphs transition? (Temporal, causal, associative, contrast)

### Punctuation habits
- Em dash frequency and usage patterns (interruption, aside, list, dramatic pause)
- Ellipsis usage (trailing off, hesitation, time passing)
- Semicolon, colon usage
- Parenthetical frequency

### Dialogue mechanics
- Dialogue tag patterns (said, asked, vs action beats, vs untagged)
- Ratio of dialogue to narration in conversation scenes
- How internal reaction interleaves with spoken dialogue

### Repetition and motifs
- Repeated phrases or constructions across chapters
- Callback patterns (references to earlier chapters/events)
- Any verbal tics in the narration itself

### Chapter structure
- How do chapters typically open?
- How do they close?
- Average scene count per chapter
- How are scene breaks signaled?

## Output

Write your analysis to: .meridian/work/style-extraction/brainstorm/mechanical-patterns.md

Be quantitative where possible. "Em dashes appear approximately X times per chapter" is more useful than "em dashes are used frequently." Include examples for every pattern you identify.

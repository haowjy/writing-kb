# Task: Analyze Amber's 1st-Person Narrative Voice

Read all chapters listed below (skip chapters 6, 13, 16 which are 3rd person). Your job is to extract the specific, concrete patterns that make Amber's narration distinctive.

## What to analyze

### Sentence-level patterns
- Typical sentence structures and how they vary
- How internal monologue is rendered (italics, direct thought, narrative questioning)
- Characteristic punctuation usage (em dashes, ellipses, parentheticals)

### The adult/child split
- How does the adult mind manifest in narration? Quote specific examples.
- How does the child body/instinct manifest? Quote specific examples.
- Where does the split create tension, humor, or pathos?

### Humor mechanics
- Catalog every distinct humor technique with 2-3 examples each
- What's the ratio of humor to straight narration? Does it shift across chapters?
- What does Amber NEVER joke about?

### Tonal range
- What's the emotional floor (darkest the narration gets)?
- What's the ceiling (most joyful/excited)?
- How does voice shift between emotional registers? Does sentence structure change?

### Pop culture / isekai references
- How are old-world references integrated? Simile? Direct comparison? Internal aside?
- How frequent are they? Do they thin out over time?
- What domains does she reference? (college, internet culture, games, media, etc.)

## Output

Write your analysis to: .meridian/work/style-extraction/brainstorm/amber-narrative-voice.md

Structure it with clear headers, and ALWAYS include direct quotes with chapter citations. The quotes ARE the analysis — without them this is just opinion.

# Task: Analyze Voice Variation Across Scene Types

Read all 17 chapters. Your job is to identify how the prose voice shifts depending on what kind of scene is happening — and whether those shifts are consistent patterns or ad hoc.

## Scene types to look for

- **Battle/action scenes** — pacing, sentence length, sensory focus, how tension builds
- **Discovery/exploration** — how Amber processes new Pokemon world elements, the isekai lens
- **Emotional/intimate scenes** — grief, connection, vulnerability. How does the prose handle weight?
- **Comedy/light scenes** — what makes these feel different from the humor woven into serious scenes?
- **Exposition/worldbuilding** — how is information delivered? Dialogue? Narration? Mixed?
- **Tension/thriller** — suspense scenes, danger, how pacing and detail shift

## What to analyze per scene type

- Sentence length distribution (short/punchy vs long/flowing)
- Sensory detail density
- Internal monologue frequency
- Humor presence/absence
- Paragraph length patterns
- Pacing techniques (time compression, real-time, slow-motion)

## Pattern consistency

- Are the voice shifts deliberate and repeatable, or do they vary chapter to chapter?
- Which scene types have the most distinctive voice? The least?
- Are there scenes that mix types effectively?

## Output

Write your analysis to: .meridian/work/style-extraction/brainstorm/scene-type-variation.md

Organize by scene type. Quote specific passages showing each pattern, with chapter citations.
